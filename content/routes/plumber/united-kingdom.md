## Can South African plumbers work in the UK?

**Verdict: Medium-High viability — solid demand, clear visa, needs a sponsor.**
Yes. The usual route for a South African plumber is the **Skilled Worker visa** — a UK work visa
for people with a job offer from a Home-Office-licensed sponsor, in an eligible occupation, at or
above a set salary. You'll need a **Certificate of Sponsorship**, a salary of at least
**£41,700/yr or the going rate** (whichever is higher), and **English at level B2**. General
plumbing isn't nationally licensed, but **gas work legally requires Gas Safe Register
registration**. The visa runs up to **5 years** and can lead to **settlement**. Best suited to
qualified, experienced plumbers who can land a sponsoring employer; not suited to anyone without
a sponsor or paying for a "guaranteed" placement.

> **Warning:** Verify the employer holds a real sponsor licence (public register). Never pay an
> upfront "guaranteed job/visa" fee — and never do gas work without Gas Safe registration.

## Who is this route right for?

This fits a **qualified, experienced plumber** who can secure a UK employer with a **sponsor
licence** paying at or above the salary rule. It suits people wanting a route toward settlement,
and especially those with **gas qualifications** willing to register with Gas Safe. It is **not**
for someone without a recognised qualification or a sponsoring employer.

## What are the minimum requirements?

- A **job offer from a licensed UK sponsor** and a **Certificate of Sponsorship**.
- Salary of at least **£41,700/yr or the going rate** (£33,400 floor in some cases).
- **English at level B2** (SA not exempt).
- **£1,270 in savings** for 28 days (unless exempt).
- Valid passport, SAPS police clearance, recognised plumbing qualifications/experience.
- For gas work: **Gas Safe Register** registration.

## Which visa do you need?

The **Skilled Worker visa** — apply within 3 months of receiving your Certificate of
Sponsorship; you can't change employer without updating the visa. On the trade: general plumbing
isn't nationally licensed, but **gas work must be done by a Gas Safe-registered engineer** (it's
illegal otherwise). Confirm current rules and verify the visa details at
[gov.uk/skilled-worker-visa](https://www.gov.uk/skilled-worker-visa).

## Is the salary / offer realistic?

Check the offer clears **£41,700 or the going rate**, names a licensed sponsor, and is clear on
hours and deductions. If the role involves gas, confirm how Gas Safe registration is handled. A
big quoted rate means nothing without a licensed sponsor and a written contract.

## What scams target this route?

Red flags:

- Upfront "placement" or "guaranteed visa" fees.
- A "sponsor" not on the Home Office register.
- Anyone telling you gas work doesn't need Gas Safe registration.
- Pressure to pay before any Certificate of Sponsorship.
