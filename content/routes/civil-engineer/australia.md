## Can South African civil engineers work in Australia?

**Verdict: High–Medium viability — strong demand, with a skills assessment as the gate.**
Yes. Civil engineering is an assessable occupation for Australian skilled migration, and demand
is solid. The gate is the **Migration Skills Assessment (MSA)** from **Engineers Australia** —
you need a positive assessment **before** you apply for a visa. A Migration Skills Assessment is
Engineers Australia's official check that your qualifications and competencies meet the
Australian standard. If your degree is accredited under the **Washington Accord**, you use the
quicker accredited pathway; otherwise you prepare a **Competency Demonstration Report (CDR)**.
After a positive assessment you can be **sponsored on the Skills in Demand visa (subclass 482)**
or pursue a **points-tested permanent route (189/190)**. Best suited to degree-qualified civil
engineers; the main effort is the assessment and (for SA applicants) an English test.

> **Warning:** Engineers Australia does not award migration points or give visa advice, and no
> legitimate body guarantees a visa. Be wary of agents charging large fees for "guaranteed" PR.

## Who is this route right for?

This fits a **degree-qualified civil engineer** (the Professional Engineer category needs a
4-year accredited engineering degree) willing to complete a skills assessment and an English
test. It suits people targeting either employer sponsorship or points-based PR. It is **not**
for someone without an accredited engineering degree, or anyone expecting to migrate without the
Engineers Australia assessment — it is the foundation step for every engineering visa route.

## What are the minimum requirements?

- A relevant **engineering degree** (Professional Engineer = 4-year accredited degree).
- A **positive Engineers Australia Migration Skills Assessment** (Washington Accord pathway or
  CDR).
- An **approved English test** (SA applicants are not exempt) taken within 3 years before
  lodging — confirm current minimum scores with Engineers Australia.
- For sponsored work: an employer/sponsor (subclass 482). For PR: meeting the points test.
- Valid passport, SAPS police clearance, and supporting documents.

## Which pathway and visa do you need?

**Step 1 — the assessment.** Engineers Australia is the authorised assessing authority. Two
routes: an **accredited pathway** (if your qualification is accredited under the Washington,
Sydney or Dublin Accord) which **avoids the CDR**; or the **CDR pathway** (three career episodes
demonstrating the required competencies plus a summary statement and CPD). **South African
(ECSA) degrees may qualify under the Washington Accord, but this depends on your specific
programme and graduation year — confirm via the IEA qualification checker before assuming you
can skip the CDR.**

**Step 2 — the visa.** With a positive assessment, the common routes are the **Skills in Demand
visa (subclass 482)** (employer-sponsored) or the points-tested **subclass 189/190** permanent
routes. **We could not verify the current subclass numbers, points thresholds, or visa fees
against Home Affairs in this session (the site blocks our reader) — confirm these directly on
the official Home Affairs website before relying on them.**

## Is the salary / offer realistic?

For a sponsored role, check the offer against Australian engineering pay, that the employer is a
genuine sponsor, and the contract terms. For a points-based move you won't have an employer up
front — focus instead on whether your assessment, English score and points add up. Be wary of
"agents" promising guaranteed PR for a large fee; the assessment and visa decisions are made by
official bodies, not agents.

## What scams target this route?

Engineering scams usually involve **migration agents** over-promising. Red flags:

- "Guaranteed PR" or "guaranteed visa" for a large upfront fee.
- Anyone offering to write a CDR that misrepresents your experience (this can lead to a fraud
  finding and a ban).
- Pressure to pay before any official step.

Read our [work-abroad scam warnings](/scam-warnings). Use only registered migration agents and
verify them independently.
