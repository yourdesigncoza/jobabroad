# WhatsApp Follow-Up Templates

> **Runtime source:** [`lib/whatsapp-templates.ts`](../lib/whatsapp-templates.ts) — the TypeScript module is what `/admin` and any other UI render. This markdown doc is the editorial / review record. Any wording change here must also land in `lib/whatsapp-templates.ts` (or vice versa).

Working document. Add comments, strike through what doesn't work, replace copy directly.

---

## Context

- Person received a flyer → visited site → clicked a category tile → WhatsApp opened with a pre-filled message
- Pre-fill format: *"Hi, I'm interested in [Category] work abroad. Can you help me?"*
- They sent the message. This is our **first reply**.
- Goal: close the R199 Playbook sale — without giving away paid content
- Tone: warm, direct, honest. Not salesy. Not corporate.

---

## The Rule

**Tease the destination. Tease the complexity. Don't give the route.**

What we reveal:
- That their field has real overseas demand (which countries — no specifics)
- That the process is more involved than they think (without explaining how)
- That scams are active in their space (without listing them)

What we hold back:
- Specific visa names or routes
- Document lists
- Cost figures
- Recruiter names or agency contacts

---

## Message Flow

Every conversation runs in exactly 3 messages:

- **Message 1** — universal qualifier (same for everyone, sent immediately)
- **Message 2** — category-specific value pitch (sent after they reply with their field + experience)
- **Message 3** — PayShap details (sent only after they say yes)

---

## Message 1 — Universal Qualifier

Use this for every incoming message, regardless of which category they clicked.

> Hi 👋 Thanks for reaching out.
>
> Before I point you anywhere — what work do you do and how many years' experience do you have?
>
> I just want to make sure there's a realistic route before you pay for anything.

### Gemini Review (2026-05-02)

#### 1. Tone and Trust
- Overall tone is good — feels like a "knowledgeable cousin" rather than a corporate script.
- **"Good timing"** (used in Healthcare and IT) is a classic sales trope. If someone sends at 2 AM and gets "Good timing," they know it's a script.
- Suggestion: replace with something observational — *"You're looking at the right field"* or *"That's a very active space right now."*

#### 2. Length
- **Borderline too long.** On a budget Samsung or Hisense (common in SA), these messages fill the screen or require a scroll.
- Fix: trim the Playbook bullet list from 7 to 4–5. Combine "Visa route overview" and "Step-by-step documents." Move "CV template" out of the list — mention it as a bonus on the price line instead.

#### 3. Conversion
- R199 is the right price — impulse buy territory, but high enough to feel like proprietary information.
- **"Personal WhatsApp feedback" is the strongest conversion lever.** People pay for the guide but they *buy* the human access.
- The CTA "Want to go ahead?" is passive. Replace with something more direct: *"Ready to see the actual routes and costs? I'll send the PayShap details."*

#### 4. Trust killers to fix
- **"Legitimate contacts"** — sounds like "I'm selling you phone numbers of recruiters who won't answer." Replace with *"Direct links to official government portals."* "Official" and "verified" outperform "legitimate."
- **💚 emoji** — green heart emojis are heavily associated with SA "Forex Pastor" and grant-helper scams. Switch to something more neutral: 🔎, 🇿🇦, or 📄. Avoid the green heart.

#### 5. What's missing
- **Who is sending this?** A single trust sentence helps: *"I started Jobabroad because I was tired of watching South Africans get scammed by fake agents."* This humanises the reply and gives a reason to trust.
- **Proof of the product.** A screenshot or short clip of someone scrolling through the Playbook would kill the "is this just a Google search in a PDF?" fear. (Not urgent — but worth noting for later.)

#### 6. Category-specific notes
- **Healthcare** — strong. Mentioning UK/AUS registration routes is the right hook.
- **Trades** — needs to be grittier. Mentioning Red Seal recognition or Trade Test equivalency specifically would prove this isn't a generalist guide.
- **Farming** — highest scam-risk category. Must explicitly state that the UK Seasonal Worker Visa does NOT require an upfront fee paid to any agent. That is the most common farming scam and it needs to be called out by name.

#### 7. The "Not a Good Fit" closer
- Gemini called this a **masterstroke** — refusing the R199 wins a fan who tells 5 friends.
- One refinement: instead of "If things change, come back," give them one free homework task. *"Get your [specific document] sorted first — that will save you the most time when you're ready."*

#### Gemini's brutal summary
> The templates are 90% there. Kill the salesy openers, cut 2 bullets from the list, swap the green heart for a more expert/researcher emoji. The honesty in the "Not a good fit" section is the secret weapon — keep it exactly as is.

### ChatGPT Review (2026-05-02)

Yes — append it, but **tighten it before you use it live**.

My honest take: the document is solid, but the current templates are **slightly too long and slightly too eager to sell**. For WhatsApp, shorter will convert better.

The Gemini review is mostly right. Especially these points:

* Replace **"Good timing"** — it sounds scripted.
* Remove the **💚** — too scam-adjacent for this market.
* Replace **"Legitimate contacts"** with **"official government and programme links."**
* Keep the **"not a good fit"** reply. That is a trust weapon.
* Reduce the bullet list. Seven bullets is too much for WhatsApp.

#### The main thing I would change

Do **not** jump straight to the sale after the first message.

First ask **one qualifying question**. This makes it feel real and gives you permission to sell.

Use this as your first reply:

> Hi 👋 Thanks for reaching out.
>
> Before I point you anywhere, what work do you do and how many years' experience do you have?
>
> I just want to check that there's a realistic route before you pay for anything.

Then, once they answer, send the category reply.
