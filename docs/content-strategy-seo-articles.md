# SEO Content Strategy — Next 10 Articles

> Comparative analysis + trend research to stand up a top-of-funnel content layer for jobabroad.co.za and drive organic traffic into registration.

## Current state

The site has **no blog or article section**. The only public, indexable pages are:

- `/` (landing)
- `/recruiters`
- `/scam-warnings`
- 11 × `/pathways/[category]` pathway previews

Every page is bottom-of-funnel — it assumes the visitor already knows they want to work abroad and is choosing a category. There is **zero content capturing the much larger informational-search audience** ("how do I…", "can I…", "is it worth it…"). That audience is where the cheap, compounding organic traffic lives. These 10 articles are the fix.

| Competitor type | Examples | What they cover | Weakness Jobabroad exploits |

| **Profession recruiters** | Edvectus, MoveUp, MMA, Caredemy, SWA Nursing, Timeplan | Single-route, profession-specific recruitment pages | Salesy, thin, **one route only** — they won't tell you the alternatives |
| **Immigration consultancies** | Sable International, Intergate, IBN | Visa categories + financial/tax emigration; service is R5k–R30k | Sable's blog skews to *financial* emigration; not profession-pathway-led; premium-priced |

| **TEFL / au pair agencies** | International TEFL Academy, Cultural Care, Carla's Au Pairs | Their own niche programs | Single-program; no cross-route comparison |

### What nobody owns (Jobabroad's wedge)

1. **Profession × destination *comparison*** — route-agnostic ("a SA nurse's 3 best countries", not one recruiter's pitch).
2. **Salary-in-rands data content** — flagged as a research gap; high commercial intent; highly shareable.
3. **"No degree" routes consolidated** — search results explicitly show this is underserved.
4. **An honest, free scam/vetting guide** tied to a free eligibility assessment.

**Structural advantage:** Jobabroad already has **11 completed, deeply-researched pathway guides** (67KB–191KB each). It can publish genuinely deep, multi-route, profession-specific articles that recruiters won't write and consultancies don't bother with at the free tier — then funnel readers into a free assessment competitors don't offer.

## Pillar pages & cluster structure

A **pillar page** (a.k.a. hub) is the broad anchor page of a topic cluster. A **spoke** article goes deep on one narrow question and links *up* to its pillar. Google rewards this shape: a tight, interlinked cluster signals topical authority, and internal links pass ranking value up to the pillar.

**Jobabroad already has its pillar pages — no need to build new ones:**

| Pillar page (hub) | Role |
|---|---|
| `/` (homepage) | Top-level pillar — "work abroad from South Africa" |
| `/pathways/[category]` × 11 | Category pillars — the comprehensive per-profession guides |
| `/scam-warnings` | Safety pillar |
| `[3]` Best Countries (new article) | A blog-level sub-hub for the destination-decision cluster |

The 10 articles below are **spokes**, not pillars. Each links up to one pillar page — see the "Links up to" column — and the pillar links back down to it.

### Per-article detail

**1. How SA Nurses Can Work in the UK in 2026 (Step-by-Step)** — *Searchable.* Spoke of the `/pathways/healthcare` pillar. Flagship: nursing is the #1 demand wedge (1 in 5 SA healthcare workers has taken steps to emigrate). Cover SANC Certificate of Good Standing → NMC CCPS → OSCE → IELTS/OET → Health & Care Worker Visa. Hook: NMC applications up 4×; SA not on the WHO Red List. CTA: free healthcare eligibility check.

**2. Teach in the UK from SA: The Free QTS Route (2026)** — *Searchable.* Strategically the highest-value funnel — teaching is the **R495 paid-pilot category**, so every registration here can monetise. Hook: DfE made QTS applications free (Aug 2025), removing a major cost barrier. Cover SACE Certificate of Good Standing → "Apply for QTS in England" service → Licensed Sponsor school → Skilled Worker Visa. **Accuracy note:** QTS for SA teachers is *assessment-based, not automatic* — qualification level and subject matter, and live sources conflict on exactly who is eligible. Teachers can work as an Overseas Trained Teacher for up to 4 years and must gain QTS within that window. The article must verify current eligibility against gov.uk and frame it as "apply and be assessed", not guaranteed.

**3. Best Countries for South Africans to Work Abroad in 2026 (by Profession)** — *Searchable + shareable.* **Cornerstone hub** — beats the generic listicles by being profession-aware. Ranks UK / Australia / Ireland / Canada / Germany / NZ / Gulf and says which profession each suits. Internal-links to every profession article. Big head-term traffic.

**4. Working Abroad Without a Degree: 6 Real Routes for South Africans** — *Searchable + shareable.* Search results show this is underserved. Covers au pair (J-1), TEFL, seasonal/J-1, farming schemes, trades, hospitality — funnels into six categories from one article. Large, motivated, under-served audience.

**5. Au Pair in America from SA: Costs, Visa & How to Apply (2026)** — *Searchable.* Au pair is the most accessible structured route for SA women 18–26; strong existing search demand; agency content is thin. Cover J-1 visa, designated sponsors, 200-hr childcare requirement, $220.50/wk stipend, real costs.

**6. Recruitment Scams Targeting South Africans: 9 Red Flags** — *Searchable + shareable.* Extends `/scam-warnings`. Scam fear is the audience's #1 objection (it's baked into the WhatsApp nurture flow). Cover upfront-fee red flags, the SA "R1 only" law, how to check the UK Licensed Sponsor Register. Pure trust-builder.

**7. How Much More Do SA Nurses Earn Abroad? Salary in Rands** — *Shareable, data-driven.* Fills a documented research gap. UK Band 5 ≈ R600k+ vs SA average ≈ R342k. High commercial intent ("is it worth it"). Comparison tables UK / Australia / Gulf, take-home after tax.

**8. Best Route for SA IT Professionals: Ireland vs Germany vs Canada** — *Searchable.* Covers demand wedge #2. Ireland CSEP (€40,904, no language test), Germany Opportunity Card (non-regulated IT skips qualification recognition), Canada STEM Express Entry. Consideration-stage comparison.

**9. Police Clearance, Apostille & SAQA: The Document Checklist (2026)** — *Searchable.* Implementation-stage, evergreen. Concierges own pieces of this; nobody offers a free unified sequence. The "leaving document prep too late" mistake is the most common avoidable failure. Strong assessment CTA.

**10. UK Ancestry Visa vs Skilled Worker Visa: Which Route Is Right?** — *Searchable.* Route comparison. Ancestry visa (UK-born grandparent, no sponsor, no salary floor) is the first choice for many of the 250k-strong SA diaspora — and the Jul 2025 Skilled Worker tightening makes it relatively more attractive. Timely comparison angle.

## Architecture recommendation

No blog exists, so this needs a home. Recommendation:

- **Section:** `/blog/` (unambiguous; "guide" already means the gated member pathway content, `/pathways/` the public preview — avoid collision).
- **URLs:** `/blog/[slug]` with keyword-led slugs (e.g. `/blog/nursing-jobs-uk-from-south-africa`).
- **Render:** reuse the existing markdown pipeline — `content/blog/[slug].md` rendered via the `lib/pathway-content.ts` sanitiser/renderer (already forces `target="_blank"` on external links per the project rule).
- **Sitemap:** add a `blogRoutes` block to `app/sitemap.ts` alongside `pathwayRoutes`, `changeFrequency: 'monthly'`.
- **Each post:** OG card (mirror `app/pathways/[category]/opengraph-image.tsx`), Article + FAQ JSON-LD, author/date meta, one clear primary CTA.

## CTA / funnel

Every article ends with a single primary CTA — **"Check your eligibility free"** → `/register?category=X` → free pathway guide + assessment. Teaching articles (2, and teaching mentions in 3/7) carry extra weight: that category routes into the R495 paid pilot.
