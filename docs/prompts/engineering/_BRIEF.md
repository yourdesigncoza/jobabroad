## Professional regulatory bodies

*Which bodies regulate engineering in each destination? Which SA body handles credential recognition?*

| Country | Regulator / assessing body | SA equivalent / recognition path |
|---|---|---|
| Australia | **Engineers Australia (EA)** — the Migration Skills Assessment authority for engineering occupations. State-level Pr Eng registration is a post-arrival matter, not a migration prerequisite. | ECSA-accredited degree → EA Accord pathway (Washington / Sydney / Dublin) or CDR pathway. |
| Ireland | **Engineers Ireland** — professional title recognition. Not mandatory for the Critical Skills Employment Permit, but strengthens the application. | ECSA-accredited degree + job offer; DETE issues the permit. |
| United Kingdom | **Engineering Council UK (EngC)** — Chartered/Incorporated Engineer registration. Optional; employer sponsorship is directly accessible without it. | ECSA-accredited degree (Washington Accord recognised by ECUK); SAICE↔ICE Mutual Exemption Agreement for Professional Engineers. |
| New Zealand | **Engineering New Zealand** — registration (e.g. CPEng) where the role requires it. | ECSA-accredited degree; International Qualification Assessment (IQA) where the SA qualification is not automatically recognised. |
| Canada | **Provincial engineering regulators** (e.g. PEO Ontario, APEGA Alberta) issue P.Eng — a **post-arrival** licensing step, not a pre-arrival barrier. **WES** does the Educational Credential Assessment for Express Entry. | ECSA-accredited degree → WES ECA → Express Entry pool. |

**SA body: ECSA — Engineering Council of South Africa.** Statutory body under the Engineering Profession Act 46 of 2000; the only body authorised to register engineering professionals in SA and confer the titles Pr Eng, Pr Tech Eng, Pr Techni Eng, Pr Cert Eng. **Critical distinction for engineers:** unlike nurses (SANC) or teachers (SACE), recognition abroad is carried primarily by the **ECSA accreditation of the degree** (via the international Accords), not by personal Pr Eng registration. Most SA engineers do not need to be registered as a Pr Eng to emigrate — but should confirm whether a specific destination assessor wants an ECSA confirmation/good-standing letter.

## Known scam patterns

*Scams that specifically target SA engineering workers.*

- **Inflated "CDR writing service" scams** — migration agents charging large fees to write a Competency Demonstration Report when the SA engineer's ECSA-accredited degree actually qualifies for the cheaper EA **Accord pathway** and needs no CDR at all. The single most engineering-specific scam: it preys on candidates not knowing which EA pathway they qualify for.
- **"Guaranteed engineering job + visa sponsorship" upfront-fee fraud** — fake UK/Australian recruiters offering a confirmed job and charging for visa processing, training, or flight deposits. Verify any UK employer on the UK Register of Licensed Sponsors before paying anyone.
- **Maritime / oil-rig / mining engineering job-offer scams** — the Australian High Commission Pretoria has issued an official warning about maritime employment scams targeting South Africans.
- **Fake "fast-track Engineers Australia assessment" intermediaries** — third parties claiming they can expedite or guarantee an EA outcome; EA's only paid fast-track is its own portal add-on.
- **Requests for ID documents, passport copies, or bank details before any signed contract** — generic but heavily used against engineers responding to overseas listings.

## Legitimate programmes / bilateral agreements

*Official SA–destination agreements for this profession — these ARE the differentiator.*

- **Washington Accord** — ECSA signatory since 1999. Mutual recognition of **4-year professional engineering (BEng) degrees** among signatories including Australia, Canada, New Zealand, UK, USA, Ireland. This is what makes the EA Accord pathway available to most SA engineers.
- **Sydney Accord** — mutual recognition of **3-year engineering technologist** qualifications (BEngTech / BTech).
- **Dublin Accord** — mutual recognition of **engineering technician** qualifications.
- **SAICE ↔ ICE (UK) Mutual Exemption Agreement** — SAICE members registered with ECSA as Professional Engineers (1+ year standing) can obtain corresponding ICE membership/registration in the UK.
- No single SA-government "engineer placement programme" exists — the Accords are the legitimate recognition mechanism, and they are publicly verifiable at ieagreements.org.
