## Buyer profile

A South African ICT professional — typically degree-qualified (BSc Comp Sci, BCom Informatics, BEng Computer Engineering, or NQF 6+ diploma), in a software development, data engineering, cloud / DevOps, cybersecurity, or IT-architect role — earning R450k–R1.2M/yr in SA, frustrated by Rand devaluation and load-shedding pressure on home tech work. They are NOT registered with a regulator (IT is non-regulated in SA) and may be unsure whether their qualifications are accepted abroad.

## Professional regulatory bodies

*ICT is a NON-REGULATED profession in most destinations — this is the central feasibility advantage. Skills assessment / qualification recognition still applies in some routes.*

| Country | "Regulator" / assessment body | SA equivalent / recognition path |
|---|---|---|
| Ireland | DETE (Department of Enterprise, Tourism and Employment) — issues permits; no professional regulator | SA degree accepted directly by DETE for CSEP listed-occupation roles; no separate skills assessment |
| UK | UK Visas and Immigration (UKVI) — Skilled Worker route; SOC 2020 occupation codes; no professional regulator | UK NARIC / Ecctis statement of comparability optional but rarely required; employer's licensed sponsor status is the gate |
| Germany | ZAB (Zentralstelle für ausländisches Bildungswesen) — but **ICT is non-regulated**; ZAB recommended only to score points | Anabin database lookup for the SA university; if listed H+ then degree is recognised; Statement of Comparability ≈ €200, 4–12 weeks |
| Canada | WES (World Education Services) ECA for Express Entry CRS points | WES ECA for SA degrees: CAD$265, 7–12 weeks; Canadian bachelor equivalent typical for SA 3-yr BSc |
| Australia | ACS (Australian Computer Society) — skills assessment for 482 / 186 ICT applications | ACS Skills Assessment for SA degrees AUD$550 (Post-Australian Study) / AUD$610 (Skills Assessment); 6–16 weeks |

**SA side:** No SA professional body for IT. **IITPSA (Institute of Information Technology Professionals South Africa)** is the closest analogue — voluntary professional membership, not a statutory regulator. Most SA ICT workers are NOT members.

**SAQA evaluation:** Optional for SA-issued degrees when applying overseas; primarily for ZAB / WES / ACS as a starting document.

## Known scam patterns

*Scams that specifically target SA ICT workers (not generic visa scams):*

- **Fake "guaranteed Germany IT job" packages** — agents charging R20–50k upfront for "Opportunity Card application + guaranteed placement"; no agent can guarantee a job, and the Opportunity Card application itself costs €75 visa fee + blocked-account funds only
- **Fake CSEP "fast-track" offers** — claims of paid expedite via "DETE insiders"; Trusted Partner status is held by employers, not paid for by candidates
- **Recruiter signing "exclusive representation" demanding a percentage of first salary** — legitimate Ireland and UK tech recruiters are paid by the employer, never by the candidate
- **"You won the UK Tech Visa lottery" emails** — there is no UK tech lottery; the Skilled Worker Visa and Global Talent are application-based
- **Fake Microsoft / Google "remote-then-relocate" interview invitations** — typically via WhatsApp; ask candidates to install software (information-stealer malware) and pay for "relocation kit"
- **Fake LinkedIn Easy Apply auto-spam from "Sydney recruiters"** asking for SA ID + bank statement before any interview
- **"Crypto-trading IT support roles" in Dubai / Cyprus** — many are unregulated forex/crypto operations; some are organised-crime recruitment fronts (KK Park, Myanmar-style scam compounds)

## Legitimate programmes / bilateral agreements

*Official SA–destination programmes or recognition arrangements for ICT:*

- **Ireland CSEP** — open to SA passport-holders directly; no bilateral agreement needed
- **Sydney Accord / Washington Accord / Dublin Accord** — SA ECSA is a signatory, but these cover **engineering**, not pure ICT; relevant only for SA Computer Engineering BEng grads going to Engineers Australia route
- **WES recognised SA universities** — Canada accepts SA bachelor degrees from publicly recognised SA universities (UCT, Wits, Stellenbosch, UP, UJ, UKZN, etc.) as ECA-equivalent to Canadian bachelor's
- **No SA–Germany bilateral programme** specific to ICT; Opportunity Card applies globally

## Notes for prompt generation

*Domain-specific facts the vault prompts must flag / confirm / avoid:*

- **Date-stamp every salary threshold and visa fee.** Ireland CSEP and UK Skilled Worker thresholds change yearly or twice yearly; print issue date on every figure.
- **Confirm 2026 status** of: UK B2 English requirement (effective 8 Jan 2026), Ireland salary thresholds (effective 1 Mar 2026), UK TSL expiry (31 Dec 2026 unless renewed).
- **ICT is non-regulated almost everywhere** — call this out explicitly. SA ICT workers often assume they need a skills assessment for every country; only Canada (WES) and Australia (ACS) actually require one. Ireland, UK, and Germany do NOT require formal ICT-specific assessment.
- **IT roles in Germany bypass ZAB qualification recognition** — non-regulated profession route. Highlight this; it's the biggest unknown advantage for SA candidates.
- **WES ECA only validates the degree at a Canadian-equivalent level**, not the right to work — many SA candidates conflate the two.
- **UK SOC code mapping** must be specific: SA "Software Developer" maps to SOC 2134 (programmers and software development professionals) — this is the eligible SOC code. SA "IT Support" maps to SOC 3132/3133 (RQF 3–5) — generally NOT eligible for Skilled Worker since July 2025 unless on the Temporary Shortage List (TSL).
- **Never quote a salary in ZAR** as a destination-currency figure — always cite the local currency from the official source.
- **Australia ACS assessment vs Engineers Australia** — Computer Engineering BEng grads can sometimes choose either path; ACS is faster and cheaper for software roles. Cite ACS as the primary route for ICT.
- **No SA government bilateral / "labour mobility" agreement for ICT** — do not invent one. Routes are individual-application-based.
- **Cite primary sources only** — single non-primary citation = `<!-- TODO: human-review: unverified -->` flag (per buyer-north-star.md rule).
- **Flag closed routes:** care worker UK route (irrelevant to ICT but commonly confused), Australia 489 visa (closed long ago, replaced by 491 / state nomination — irrelevant to most ICT).
