# Final Report: wa-tefl-05-scams

## Goal

Build a TEFL-specific reference of scam and fraud patterns targeting South African TEFL applicants in 2025–2026 — covering recruiter fraud, fake guaranteed placement course-seller packages, unaccredited TEFL certificates, passport-holding on arrival, mid-contract school closures (China/Vietnam), and the verification tools an SA candidate can use to confirm a legitimate employer, programme, or certificate.

## Conclusion

→ [[Conclusion]] — direct answer: six documented scam patterns, five with SA-specific angles; seven official verification tools; Employment Services Act Clause 15 makes SA placement fees illegal; Spain's Auxiliares programme is not open to SA citizens.

## What Was Discovered

**Six scam patterns** documented with TEFL-specific and SA-specific angles:

1. **[[Scam Patterns/Guaranteed Placement Package Scam]]** — SA-based "placement agents" charging R5,000–R30,000 for Korea/Japan/China placements that could be obtained for free. Directly violates SA Employment Services Act Clause 15 (private employment agencies prohibited from charging work seekers fees). EPIK and JET applications are free; official recruiters for these programmes are paid by the destination government.

2. **[[Scam Patterns/Unaccredited TEFL Certificate Scam]]** — The "regulated vs. accredited" confusion exploited by providers claiming Ofqual status without Cambridge CELTA or Trinity CertTESOL accreditation. The TEFL Academy (TTA) case is documented: certificates rejected outside UK/Ireland including reportedly in Ireland itself. Vietnam under Decree 219/2025/ND-CP requires minimum 120hr TEFL; UAE and Saudi require CELTA-equivalent for most licensed positions.

3. **[[Scam Patterns/Upfront Visa Processing Fee Scam]]** — The iEnglish Language Institute Dubai variant is active since 2018 and still active as of January 2026, cycling through aliases (Rashid Language School, Wison/Wilson Recruitment, Rubaj Recruitment). Fantasy job offers ($4,800/month tax-free, free housing + car + family flights) followed by "$200–$750 visa processing fee" to a Western Union payment address. The Saudi and Vietnam variants follow the same structure.

4. **[[Scam Patterns/Fake EPIK SMOE JET Recruiter Scam]]** — SA agents impersonating or misrepresenting EPIK/SMOE/JET programme affiliation. SA is an eligible JET participant country (Embassy of Japan in SA handles applications directly, free, deadline October). EPIK has an official recruiter list — no SA-based agent appears on it.

5. **[[Scam Patterns/Passport Holding on Arrival]]** — Legitimate employers need the passport briefly for work permit lodgement; they must return it within days. Indefinite passport retention at lower-tier Chinese, Vietnamese, and Cambodian private academies is a serious labour exploitation indicator and potential trafficking precursor. Report to DIRCO and the SA Embassy in the destination country.

6. **[[Scam Patterns/Mid-Contract Chinese Private School Closure Scam]]** — China's Double Reduction Policy (July 2021) destroyed the private tutoring sector. Mid-contract closures have been chronic since 2021. Two employer verification tools (fuwu.most.gov.cn and gsxt.gov.cn) can detect non-viable employers before signing. The pattern will continue as long as non-compliant private academies attempt to recruit foreign teachers.

**Three verification methods** were found to be the highest-priority for SA candidates:
- Cambridge CELTA centre check at cambridgeenglish.org (5 minutes, prevents £3,000–R15,000 wasted on unrecognised certs)
- EPIK official recruiter list check at epik.go.kr (5 minutes, prevents R5,000–R20,000 placement fee fraud)
- UAE MOHRE + KHDA check at mohre.gov.ae and khda.gov.ae (10 minutes, prevents $200–$750 visa fee extraction)

**Critical SA-specific legal finding**: SA Employment Services Act No. 4 of 2014, Clause 15, explicitly prohibits private employment agencies from charging work seekers any fees for services rendered (confirmed by SA Department of Labour). SA candidates who have paid a TEFL placement fee to a local agent have a legal basis for complaint to the Department of Employment and Labour.

**Spain finding**: The Auxiliares de Conversación programme requires citizenship from a country with a signed MOU with Spain. South Africa is NOT on the 2025-2026 or 2026-2027 eligible countries list. Any SA agent claiming to place SA nationals as Auxiliares is selling a fraudulent product.

## Gaps That Remain Open

2. **Cambodia official warning**: Passport holding pattern in Cambodia is community-documented (alleged) but no official government warning (embassy, SAPS, DIRCO) was found naming Cambodia specifically. Additional research via SA Embassy in Cambodia or official AU/NZ government travel warnings may surface an official citation.

3. **SAFPS TEFL-specific fraud records**: SAFPS (safps.org.za) covers financial fraud broadly. No TEFL-specific fraud bulletins were found in public SAFPS data. A direct query to SAFPS or Yima (yima.org.za) may surface SA-specific TEFL fraud case statistics.

4. **Named SA TEFL placement agents in regulatory/court records**: No individual SA agent was found named in a government warning or court action. The Employment Services Act violation pattern is confirmed as a category but no named SA agent could be cited. Department of Labour prosecution records are not publicly accessible.
