# Final Report: wa-tefl-02-documents

## Goal

Build a sequenced, source-verified document checklist for a South African TEFL applicant — covering every document from SA-side preparation through TEFL certificate accreditation through destination-side apostille and embassy attestation, with realistic processing times, costs, and the specific traps that delay SA applicants for Korea, the Gulf, Vietnam, and China.

## Conclusion

→ [[Conclusion]] — direct answer to the research goal

**SA TEFL applicants need 4 core SA-side documents.** The full preparation timeline is 12–20 weeks. The Apostille Convention has simplified authentication for Korea, Saudi Arabia, China, and UAE. Vietnam is the sole exception until 11 September 2026. CELTA/CertTESOL are the only verifiably safe TEFL certificates for all five major destinations.

### The Four Core Documents

1. **SAPS Police Clearance Certificate** — R190, ~4–8 weeks (backlog), valid 6 months. Apply after job offer confirmed. Must be apostilled by DIRCO.
2. **SAQA Individual Verification Letter** — ~20 working days, fee-based (SAQA quotation). Required before DIRCO apostille for any SA degree. NOT the SAQA Evaluation Service (that is for foreign qualifications, ~90 working days).
3. **DIRCO Apostille/Authentication** — free, same-day (≤5 docs, booked). Issues Apostille Certificate for Convention countries (Korea, Saudi, China, UAE) or Certificate of Authentication for non-Convention (Vietnam pre-Sep 2026).
4. **CELTA or Trinity CertTESOL** — Ofqual Level 5, UK-issued. Verified against Cambridge English / Trinity College London registers. If apostille required (China some employers; Vietnam legalisation chain; UAE some schools): route through UK FCDO — NOT DIRCO. Cost R3,000–R7,000 and 4–6 weeks via UK.

## What Was Discovered

### Apostille Convention Status Simplifies Most Routes

The Apostille Convention now covers all major TEFL destinations except Vietnam (until September 2026). Saudi Arabia joined in December 2022 — the old chain of Notary → DIRCO → Saudi Embassy Pretoria → Saudi MOFA is no longer required; DIRCO apostille alone is sufficient. China joined in November 2023 — the old Chinese Embassy authentication chain is superseded. DIRCO apostille is now accepted by Chinese authorities, ending a significant administrative burden for SA applicants.

Vietnam remains the exception: accession was deposited on 31 December 2025, but the Convention enters into force for Vietnam only on 11 September 2026. Until that date, SA documents must go through DIRCO Certificate of Authentication + Vietnamese Embassy/Consulate Pretoria legalisation + certified Vietnamese translation — adding 2–4 weeks to the Vietnam-specific preparation.

### CELTA/CertTESOL Are UK-Issued — Critical Routing Distinction

CELTA (Cambridge Assessment English) and Trinity CertTESOL (Trinity College London) are **UK-issued qualifications**. Any apostille for these certificates must go through the UK FCDO Legalisation Office, not DIRCO. DIRCO can only apostille South African public documents. SA applicants who need apostilled TEFL certificates (China, Vietnam work permit dossier, some UAE schools) must courier originals to the UK, have them certified by a UK solicitor, and submit to the FCDO — approximately R3,000–R7,000 and 4–6 weeks roundtrip.

Not all destinations require apostilled TEFL certificates: Korea E-2 visa does not require the TEFL certificate to be apostilled (employers verify directly). Saudi Arabia generally accepts the original certificate. Always confirm with the specific employer before sending documents to the UK.

### Korea's SA-Specific Proof of English Education Requirement

South African applicants to the Korea E-2 visa and EPIK programme face a unique requirement not imposed on any other of the 7 approved nationalities: letters from every school attended from Grade 7 through university confirming English was the primary language of instruction. This is the most administratively time-consuming part of the Korea application for SA applicants — tracking down letters from institutions that may have closed, merged, or changed names over the years.

### Vietnam's Decree 219/2025 Updates

Vietnam's new work permit regime (Decree 219/2025/ND-CP, effective 7 August 2025) classifies South Africa as a "native English speaking country," meaning SA applicants need only a degree + 120-hour TEFL certificate and no English proficiency test. The experience requirement for those without TEFL was reduced from 3 years to 2 years. The employer now submits the integrated Form 03 via the National Public Service Portal, streamlining the process. Certificate verification against issuer registers is now standard under this decree — making verifiable certificates (CELTA, Trinity CertTESOL) essential.

### Saudi Arabia's 2025 Skills-Based Work Permit System

Saudi Arabia's new skills-based classification (effective July 2025 via Qiwa platform) places English teaching in "skilled/high-skilled" category. The Qiwa platform (qiwa.sa) is now the mandatory route for all employer-initiated work permit applications. The old direct MHRSD application process has been replaced. Employers must classify roles under the Saudi Standard Classification of Occupations and demonstrate Nitaqat compliance before a Visa Authorisation Number is issued.
