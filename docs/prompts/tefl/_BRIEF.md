## Audience and positioning

**Who this guide is for** — South Africans who want to teach English abroad. Two distinct sub-audiences:
1. **Degreed candidates** (any bachelor's, any field) — eligible for the visa-restricted, high-paying markets (Korea EPIK, Gulf, China, Vietnam, Japan).
2. **Non-degreed candidates** — restricted to a narrower set (some Cambodia/Vietnam private centres, online teaching, volunteer-stipend programmes).

**Distinct from `teaching` category** — the `teaching` pathway covers SACE-registered school teachers going to UK schools via QTS; this `tefl` pathway covers non-formal English teaching where SACE registration is NOT required. Do not duplicate content between the two guides; cross-reference instead.

**Positioning angle (from research):** "The most accessible work-abroad route for South Africans — no professional registration, no SAQA equivalency for the job itself, structured government programmes that explicitly list South Africa on the 7-nation native-English list." Lead with this structural SA advantage.

## Professional regulatory bodies

TEFL is **not a regulated profession** in any destination. No statutory body issues TEFL licences. The regulatory landscape is structured around:

| Country | Authority on the job side | SA equivalent / recognition path |
|---|---|---|
| **South Korea** | Korean Ministry of Justice issues E-2 visa via consulate; EPIK / GEPIK / SMOE are programme operators (not regulators) | No SA body. Degree authentication via **SAQA**. Apostille via **DIRCO**. National police clearance via **SAPS**. |
| **Saudi Arabia / UAE** | Ministry of Education (Saudi); KHDA (Dubai schools authority); MOE UAE | Same SA stack: SAQA + DIRCO + SAPS. Saudi requires MOFA attestation of degree from SA → Saudi embassy. |
| **Vietnam** | Ministry of Labour, Invalids and Social Affairs (MOLISA); Decree 219/2025/ND-CP is current work-permit framework | Apostilled SAPS + apostilled degree + apostilled TEFL cert; certified Vietnamese translations required |
| **China** | State Administration of Foreign Experts Affairs (SAFEA) → Foreign Expert Certificate → Z-visa | Same SA stack; degree + SAPS + TEFL cert all require apostille and Chinese embassy authentication |
| **Spain** | Ministerio de Educación (programme administrator) | No formal regulator; participants are programme assistants, not employees |

**SA-side body:** None for TEFL. **DIRCO** (Department of International Relations and Cooperation) apostille service is the universal SA-side touchpoint. **SAQA** evaluates the degree for some destinations.

**TEFL certificate accreditation (not a regulator, but a credential check):** Employers and visa offices increasingly verify TEFL certificates via the issuing body's accreditation register. CELTA (Cambridge), Trinity CertTESOL, and Ofqual-regulated Level 5 Diplomas are the strongest. Unverifiable certificates → visa rejection. Flag this in the documents prompt.

## Known scam patterns

Generic Fake Job Offer Scam applies (see wiki); TEFL-specific patterns:

- **"Guaranteed placement" R20–30k packages from SA-based agents** — these are CV-forwarding services, not employers. Programme application to EPIK, JET, Auxiliares, and direct hagwon hiring is **free** through the official channels.
- **Recruiter demanding upfront "visa processing fees"** — legitimate Korean EPIK/SMOE, Gulf school groups, and Vietnamese language centres pay the visa fees themselves; no candidate should pay a recruiter to process a visa.
- **Passport-holding on arrival** — common in low-end Chinese, Vietnamese, and Cambodian private academies. Employer demands the passport "for safekeeping" or to "process residence permit." Legitimate employers may need it briefly for the work permit office, but never hold it long-term. Refuse permanently surrendering the passport.
- **Private Chinese academies closing mid-contract** — chronic since 2021 policy reset; teacher loses owed salary, visa cancelled, must leave country. Mitigate by checking school's SAFEA registration and online reputation before signing.
- **Unaccredited TEFL certificates** — Vietnamese work-permit authorities and international schools verify certs against accreditation registers (Ofqual, Cambridge, etc.). "TEFL certificates" from unverifiable providers result in visa/permit rejection. Flag CELTA, Trinity CertTESOL, Ofqual Level 5 as the safe choices.
- **TEFL-cert-sellers promising "Korean job included"** — TEFL course providers are not employers and cannot guarantee jobs. Treat any "course + job guarantee" package as suspect.
- **Fake "native speaker" exemptions** — some agents claim they can secure E-2 visas for non-SA candidates by editing documents; this is fraud and results in deportation + lifetime ban.

## Legitimate programmes / bilateral agreements

- **EPIK (English Program in Korea)** — Korean Ministry of Education programme; free to apply directly; SA listed as one of seven accepted nationalities. Two annual intakes (Spring / Fall).
- **GEPIK (Gyeonggi)** — now consolidated with EPIK.
- **SMOE (Seoul Metropolitan Office of Education)** — runs Seoul-only intakes; same eligibility.
- **JET Programme (Japan)** — Japanese MOFA-run; SA eligible but recruitment SA-side is narrow.
- **Auxiliares de Conversación (Spain)** — Spanish Ministerio de Educación programme; annual SA cohort.
- **No formal SA-destination bilateral agreements** for TEFL specifically.
