## Destination shortlist

The four realistic destinations for **South African hospitality workers** are listed below. The UK Skilled Worker route was significantly narrowed on 22 July 2025 — most line-level chef and front-of-house roles were removed from eligibility. The UAE remains the most accessible destination overall.

| Country | Demand signal | Why realistic for SA |
|---|---|---|
| **United Arab Emirates (Dubai, Abu Dhabi)** | Persistent active recruitment by UAE hotel groups (Marriott, Hilton, Jumeirah, Emaar Hospitality) of SA staff; large established SA expat hospitality community; English working language | Employer-sponsored Employment Visa via UAE Federal Authority for Identity, Citizenship, Customs and Port Security (ICP) / GDRFA Dubai; no points test; SA passport holders eligible. No PR pathway. Standard 2-year contracts; employer pays all visa + Emirates ID + medical fees |
| **United Kingdom** (senior chef + management only) | UK shortage occupation list (Skilled Occupation list / Immigration Salary List) retains "Chefs" SOC 5434 only at RQF 6 / management level; entry-level chef roles removed 22 July 2025 | Skilled Worker visa for senior/head/sous chef + restaurant management roles meeting £41,700 general threshold or £33,400 reduced threshold for some new entrants; salary going-rate for SOC 5434 must be checked. CSEP (Critical Skills) routes for hotel general managers via specific employer sponsors. Most line-level UK roles now closed |
| **Australia** (skilled chef + management) | Chef (351311) on Core Skills Occupation List (CSOL); shortage signal in regional and metro hospitality; sustained Australian Skills Classification (OSCA) review pending | Skills in Demand Visa (subclass 482) Core Skills stream; CSIT (Core Skills Income Threshold) AUD $76,515 from 1 July 2025 — rising to AUD $79,499 from 1 July 2026; VETASSESS skills assessment required; pathway to subclass 186 ENS PR after 2 years |
| **Canada** (chef + cook via TFWP) | Persistent demand in tourism-heavy provinces (BC, Alberta, Quebec); chef and restaurant cook on multiple PNP occupation lists; TFWP Hospitality Stream open via LMIA-based employer sponsorship | TFWP work permit via LMIA; SAWP not applicable (agricultural); employer-driven — Canadian employer must obtain positive LMIA before SA worker can apply for work permit. Provincial Nominee Program pathway to PR for senior chef/cook positions in select provinces |

**Closed / restricted routes to flag in the guide:**
- **UK Skilled Worker for line-level cooks, kitchen porters, baristas, waiting staff** — removed from eligible occupations list 22 July 2025 (rule change). Any recruiter pitching these roles for UK Skilled Worker visa is selling a route that no longer exists.
- **USA J-1 hospitality intern/trainee** — eligible only for SA students or recent graduates with culinary training; not a permanent route; not for established hospitality professionals (covered in seasonal guide, not here).
- **UAE "freelance / sponsorship-free" hospitality work** — does not exist for line staff; freelance permit is for media/tech/consulting only. Any "work-and-find-a-job-in-Dubai-on-a-tourist-visa" pitch is a scam pattern.

## Professional regulatory bodies

Hospitality, like farming, is a **non-regulated occupation** in destination countries — there is no equivalent of a nursing board or engineering registration body for chefs or hospitality staff. Recognition runs through the visa scheme + employer + (for Australia) skills assessment.

| Country | Body / authority | Function |
|---|---|---|
| UAE | Ministry of Human Resources and Emiratisation (MOHRE) + ICP (federal) / GDRFA Dubai (emirate) | Issues work permits and Employment Visas; no separate professional registration |
| UK | Home Office (sponsor licence) + UK Visas and Immigration (UKVI) | Issues Certificates of Sponsorship under Skilled Worker route; chef occupation requires sponsor on Worker register |
| Australia | Department of Home Affairs + VETASSESS | Skills assessment for chef (351311), cook (351411), and pastry cook (351112); employer sponsorship via Standard Business Sponsorship |
| Canada | ESDC (Employment & Social Development Canada) + IRCC | ESDC issues LMIA; IRCC processes work permit; provincial cooks register may be required in regulated provinces |

**SA-side bodies (relevant to credentials, not licensing):**
- **CATHSSETA** — Sector Education and Training Authority for Culture, Arts, Tourism, Hospitality and Sport (issues NQF-aligned hospitality qualifications)
- **SAQA** — for foreign qualification evaluation when destination employer or migration authority requires NQF-level proof
- **DIRCO** — apostille on police clearance, qualifications, and trade/diploma certificates
- **City & Guilds International** — UK-recognised qualifications gateway used by some SA culinary schools (International Hotel School, Capsicum, Prue Leith)

## Primary visa routes

| Country | Visa / permit name | Status | Key requirement |
|---|---|---|---|
| UAE | **Employment Visa (Standard Work Permit)** | Open | UAE employer sponsorship; trade-licence-holding company; medical fitness test; Emirates ID; standard 2-year contract; no minimum salary threshold for hospitality roles. Employer pays MOHRE work-permit fee + visa fee + Emirates ID. Worker pays nothing (zero-fee rule under MOHRE Resolution No. 47 of 2022) |
| UK | **Skilled Worker Visa (sponsored)** | Restricted (chef/management only since 22 July 2025) | UK employer with sponsor licence; SOC code on Appendix Skilled Occupations Table 1 (RQF 6); £41,700 general salary threshold OR £33,400 new-entrant; £17.13/hour minimum; English language B1+; no public funds. Chef SOC 5434 retained but only senior roles meeting £41,700+ |
| Australia | **Skills in Demand Visa (Subclass 482) — Core Skills stream** | Open for chef (351311) | Employer-sponsored; chef on CSOL; CSIT AUD $76,515 (2025–26 program year, rising to AUD $79,499 from 1 July 2026); VETASSESS skills assessment; pathway to PR via Subclass 186 after 2 years |
| Canada | **TFWP work permit (LMIA-based)** | Open via LMIA | Canadian employer; positive LMIA from ESDC; chef NOC 62200, cook NOC 63200; LMIA fee CAD $1,000 (employer pays — workers cannot be charged); work permit + biometrics CAD $240 (worker pays); PR via PNP after meeting provincial criteria |

## Known scam patterns

Specific to SA hospitality job seekers:

- **Fake "UAE hotel job" Facebook/WhatsApp recruiter** — typical pattern: AED 8,000–15,000/month chef position, "registration fee" of R3,000–R10,000 demanded upfront before "submission to employer". Real UAE employers pay all visa costs; any upfront fee request from the worker is a scam.
- **Fake UK chef placement after 22 July 2025 rule change** — recruiters still advertising "UK Skilled Worker visa for line-level chef / kitchen porter / waiter" — these roles are no longer eligible. Any pitch for UK Skilled Worker visa with quoted salary below £33,400 is a scam.
- **Cruise ship / yacht hospitality scam** — promises of USD $3,000–5,000/month with "maritime visa" + "training fee" + "uniform deposit" before signing. Legitimate cruise lines (Carnival, Royal Caribbean, MSC) recruit via published agency partners only — verify against the line's official careers page.
- **"Fake CoS for sale" UK scam** — advertised on WhatsApp/Telegram channels offering Certificates of Sponsorship for £3,000–£8,000. CoS cannot be sold; UKVI flags purchased CoS and cancels the visa.
- **Tourist-visa-and-find-a-job Dubai pitch** — recruiters tell SA workers to fly to Dubai on a 30-day visit visa "and find a hospitality job once you're here". UAE employment requires employer-initiated work permit before entry. Working on a visit visa is a deportable offence.
- **Bogus "Canadian hotel recruitment agency" demanding LMIA fee from worker** — LMIA fees (CAD $1,000) are employer-paid; any agency requesting LMIA fee from the worker is fraudulent.

## Legitimate programmes / bilateral agreements

- No formal bilateral SA–UAE / SA–UK / SA–Australia / SA–Canada hospitality agreement exists.
- **UAE MOHRE worker-protection rules** (Resolution No. 47 of 2022) prohibit charging workers any recruitment, visa, or transportation fees — these are employer obligations.
- **UK Code of Practice for Sponsors** (Workers and Temporary Workers): sponsors cannot pass sponsorship fees to workers; certificate-of-sponsorship sale is illegal.
- **Australia FWO (Fair Work Ombudsman)** publishes hospitality minimum wages and award rates — relevant for verifying CSIT-compliant offers.

## Notes for prompt generation

- **UK 22 July 2025 rule change is the most important fact in this category.** Many existing online resources still list chef as eligible for SA workers — vault prompts must explicitly verify against current GOV.UK eligible occupations list (Appendix Skilled Occupations Table 1) and flag the 22 July 2025 cutoff.
- **UAE worker-fee prohibition** under MOHRE Resolution No. 47 of 2022 must be cited in scam section — any "registration fee" / "visa processing fee" demand is a federal violation.
- **UAE has no PR pathway for hospitality workers** — make this explicit. UAE Golden Visa is restricted to investors, exceptional talent, and certain doctors/engineers/scientists; standard hospitality workers are not eligible.
- **Australia CSIT is rising 1 July 2026 to AUD $79,499** (from AUD $76,515 since 1 July 2025) — date-stamp the figure and tell the reader to re-verify before lodging.
- **Canada chef NOC 62200 vs cook NOC 63200** — chef requires "responsible for the kitchen and overall food production"; cook is "specific roles per recipe". The NOC distinction matters for PNP eligibility and salary going-rate. Vault prompts must surface this clearly.
- **VETASSESS skills assessment for chef** is the single largest cost item in the Australia route (~AUD $1,096 for offshore Full Skills Assessment, October 2025 fee schedule) — flag for re-verification at vault-build time.
- **Cruise ship work is NOT a hospitality work-abroad route under any of these visa systems.** Cruise lines are flagged-vessel maritime employers; their recruitment is via cruise-line career portals, not visa schemes. If vault research surfaces "cruise hospitality" content, classify as adjacent (not primary) and warn about scam patterns.
- **Hotel "internships" via paid programmes** (e.g. AllianceAbroad, Cultural Vistas) are J-1 / Spanish intern visa routes for trainees — not a route for established hospitality professionals. If vault research surfaces these, classify as junior/trainee track only.
