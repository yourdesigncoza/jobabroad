## Destination shortlist

The four realistic destinations for **South African farm workers** are listed below. Two heavily-marketed Pacific schemes (Australia PALM, NZ RSE) **explicitly exclude South Africans** and must be flagged as scams when they appear in WhatsApp/Facebook recruiter pitches.

| Country | Demand signal | Why realistic for SA |
|---|---|---|
| **United Kingdom** | 42,900 Seasonal Worker visa places allocated for 2026 (41,000 horticulture + 1,900 poultry) — published in DBT/Home Office quota notices | Open to all nationalities; 6 currently-approved scheme operators per GOV.UK farm-worker guidance (as of early-2026 publication): AGRI-HR, Concordia (UK) Ltd, Fruitful Jobs, HOPS Labour Solutions Ltd, Pro-Force Limited, RE Recruitment (poultry workers only). Defra-endorsed and Home Office-licensed sponsors; GLAA labour-provider licensing is a separate check. Operator list has changed year-on-year (ethero Strategic Staffing has appeared at times) — verify the current list at vault-build time sponsor SA workers directly; English-language compatible; pre-2020 SA seasonal worker presence |
| **USA** | H-2A agricultural visa — South Africa retained on DHS/State eligible-countries list (Nov 2024 designation, with renewal expected Nov 2025); from 17 Jan 2025 nationality designation no longer strictly required (case-by-case allowed) | English-language; established SA participation in H-2A dairy, fruit, sheep-shearing roles; J1 sponsor agencies (USIT, STS, CIEE) operate SA recruitment infrastructure that is reusable for H-2A referrals |
| **Canada** | TFWP Agricultural Stream + High-Wage / Low-Wage streams open to SA via LMIA-based employer sponsorship; SA workers have been placed via Agricultural Stream | SAWP (Seasonal Agricultural Worker Program) is restricted to Mexico + Caribbean states only — SA must use Agricultural Stream or general LMIA streams, not SAWP |
| **Australia** (regional sponsorship only — NOT seasonal) | Skills in Demand Visa (482) + Skilled Regional (491) carry agricultural occupations (farm manager, livestock farmer, mixed crop farmer) on CSOL; Designated Area Migration Agreements (DAMAs) cover regional ag roles | The **PALM scheme is closed to SA** (Pacific + Timor-Leste only); SA workers must take the skilled/employer-sponsored or DAMA route — typically requires farm-management or supervisory experience, not entry-level |

**Closed routes to flag explicitly in the guide:**
- **Australia PALM (Pacific Australia Labour Mobility scheme, subclass 403)** — eligibility restricted to 9 Pacific island countries + Timor-Leste. **South Africans are not eligible.** Any "PALM scheme placement" advertised to SA workers is a scam pattern.
- **New Zealand RSE (Recognised Seasonal Employer scheme)** — restricted to specified Pacific Forum nations. **South Africans are not eligible.** Same scam pattern.
- **Canada SAWP (Seasonal Agricultural Worker Program)** — restricted to Mexico + 11 Caribbean countries. SA must use the broader Agricultural Stream / LMIA route, not SAWP.

## Professional regulatory bodies

Farming, unlike nursing or teaching, is a **non-regulated occupation** in destination countries — there is no equivalent of NMC, AHPRA, SACE, or ECSA for general farm workers. Recognition runs through the visa scheme itself (employer + LMIA / Certificate of Sponsorship) rather than a professional registration body.

| Country | Body / authority | Function |
|---|---|---|
| UK | Home Office (sponsor licence) + Defra (sectoral endorsement) + GLAA (licensing) + 5 approved scheme operators: AGRI-HR, Concordia (UK) Ltd, Fruitful Jobs, HOPS Labour Solutions, Pro-Force | Issue Certificates of Sponsorship for Seasonal Worker visa; only these approved scheme operators can recruit. Operator list changes year-on-year (ethero Strategic Staffing, RE Recruitment have appeared at times) — verify current list at vault-build time |
| USA | DOL + USCIS + DHS | DOL approves Temporary Labor Certification; USCIS approves I-129; DHS publishes annual eligible-countries list (check for SA inclusion each year) |
| Canada | ESDC (Employment & Social Development Canada) + IRCC | ESDC issues LMIA; IRCC processes work permit |
| Australia | Department of Home Affairs + DEWR (employer sponsorship side) + ACN (Australian Competency Network) for skills assessment if applying for skilled stream | Skilled stream uses VETASSESS for trade-equivalent assessment of farm manager / livestock roles |

**SA-side bodies (relevant to credentials, not licensing):**
- **DALRRD** — Department of Agriculture, Land Reform and Rural Development (sector body; no individual practitioner licensing)
- **AgriSETA** — Sector Education and Training Authority for agriculture; issues NQF-aligned qualifications (relevant for skilled-stream applications, not seasonal)
- **SAQA** — for foreign qualification evaluation when destination employer requires NQF-level proof
- **DIRCO** — apostille on police clearance, qualifications

## Known scam patterns

Farming has a **distinct and severe scam landscape** that overlaps with nursing scams but has unique features. The vault prompts must surface these explicitly.

- **"PALM scheme placement" pitch.** Recruiter on Facebook/WhatsApp offers Australian PALM seasonal work to SA. Always a scam — PALM is Pacific-only. There is no SA-eligible PALM pathway.
- **"NZ RSE harvest job" pitch.** Same pattern, NZ side. RSE is Pacific Forum nations only.
- **Upfront recruitment fee.** Real H-2A and UK Seasonal Worker visas prohibit charging recruitment fees to the worker. Any request for placement, training, or "visa processing" fee is a scam under both H-2A regulations and UK scheme operator rules.
- **"Guaranteed PR through farming".** Neither UK Seasonal Worker, H-2A, nor Canadian Agricultural Stream provides a direct PR pathway. Anyone promising PR via seasonal farm work is misrepresenting.
- **Maritime / fishing vessel scam (per Australian High Commission Pretoria warning).** Targets SA workers with fake offshore agriculture / aquaculture jobs. Documented by AHC Pretoria.
- **Fake scheme operator.** Only the small set of approved Seasonal Worker scheme operators (currently 5 — AGRI-HR, Concordia (UK) Ltd, Fruitful Jobs, HOPS Labour Solutions, Pro-Force; verify at vault-build time as the list has changed year-on-year and ethero Strategic Staffing / RE Recruitment have appeared) can issue CoS. Any other "UK farm visa agency" claiming to issue CoS is fraudulent. Vault prompts must surface the current official operator list at gov.uk.
- **"Fast-track Australian DAMA visa" pitch.** DAMAs are employer-led with state government endorsement, not consumer-facing. SA workers cannot self-apply for DAMA via a "DAMA agent".

## Legitimate programmes / bilateral agreements

There is **no SA–destination bilateral agricultural labour agreement** of the type that exists between (e.g.) Mexico and Canada (SAWP) or Pacific states and Australia (PALM). SA farm workers access destinations purely via:

1. Employer-led sponsorship (UK, Australia skilled, Canada LMIA)
2. Agency-led recruitment under licensed scheme operator structures (UK 4 operators, US H-2A agents)
3. Direct visa application after employer offer (USA H-2A, Canada Agricultural Stream)

There is **no shortcut programme** specific to SA. Vault content should explicitly state this so readers do not fall for "exclusive SA scheme" recruiter claims.

## Notes for prompt generation

Domain-specific facts the vault prompts must flag, confirm, or avoid:

1. **PALM exclusion is the single most important fact.** Every vault that touches Australia must lead with: "South Africans are not eligible for PALM." This is the most common scam vector in SA farming work-abroad space.
2. **NZ RSE exclusion** — same treatment.
3. **SAWP exclusion** for Canada — note that Canada is still accessible via Agricultural Stream and LMIA, just not via the SAWP route many recruiters reference.
4. **UK rule change Nov 2025** — 6-month max in 10-month rolling period (not 12). 4-month cooling-off for horticulture CoS issued on/after 11 Nov 2025. Re-verify at vault-build time in case further amendments.
5. **2026 UK quota: 42,900 (41,000 horticulture / 1,900 poultry)** — was 43,000/2,000 in 2025. Re-verify.
6. **H-2A SA eligibility expires 7 Nov 2025** under the current designation; check Federal Register for the renewal notice published around early-mid Nov 2025. From 17 Jan 2025 nationality designation is no longer strictly required, but the published list still strongly influences employer choices.
7. **H-2A worker fee prohibition** — workers must not be charged recruitment, transportation, or visa fees by recruiters. Any fee request is a violation and a scam signal.
8. **Australia DAMA + skilled stream require experience and skills assessment** — do not present as entry-level seasonal work. Position as a separate, longer-term, manager/livestock-skilled track.
9. **No PR via seasonal routes** — UK, H-2A, and SAWP-style Canadian seasonal routes do not lead to PR. Australian skilled (491 → 191) and Canadian high-wage LMIA routes can lead to PR but require multi-year skilled employment.
10. **SA passport visa-free / requires-visa status** — for entry to all four destinations, SA passport requires a visa. Cost the visa fee, IHS (UK), and any SEVIS/POE fee (USA H-2A) into Section 3 (Realistic Costs).
11. **Apostille requirement** — police clearance and any qualifications must be apostilled via DIRCO for all four destinations.
12. **Section 6 (Legitimate Contacts)** — list the current UK approved scheme operators (5 as of late 2025: AGRI-HR, Concordia (UK) Ltd, Fruitful Jobs, HOPS Labour Solutions, Pro-Force; verify the list at vault-build via gov.uk and note any year-on-year additions like ethero Strategic Staffing / RE Recruitment) and the J1-sponsor-equivalent SA-based H-2A recruiters (validate these at vault-build — H-2A SA agent landscape is sparser than J1 and may be fragile).
13. **Australia occupation lists are not all CSOL** — subclass 482 Core Skills uses CSOL; subclass 491 uses skilled / state occupation lists; DAMA uses agreement-specific occupation lists. Vault prompts must distinguish these and not refer to "CSOL" as the universal Australian list.
14. **Canada Agri-Food Pilot closed 14 May 2025** — do not present as an open route. Other PNP / Atlantic / Rural / Francophone community pilots may be viable for skilled agri-food workers — note these as secondary skilled pathways rather than seasonal options.
