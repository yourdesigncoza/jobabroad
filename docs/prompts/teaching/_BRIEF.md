## Known scam patterns

*What scams specifically target SA teacher workers?*

- Fake UK/Australia teaching agency on Facebook/WhatsApp claiming to secure "guaranteed placements" for an upfront "placement fee" or "visa processing fee" — legitimate recruiters do not charge teachers fees
- Fraudulent "Certificate of Sponsorship" scam: victims pay for a fake CoS number; real CoS is issued free by the sponsor employer, not for sale
- Identity document harvesting: fake QTS application portals or "SACE document assistance" services requesting original SACE certificate, passport, or bank details before any employment contract exists
- "No subject restriction" claim: scammers misrepresent QTS eligibility, claiming any SA teacher qualifies — in reality, the DfE overseas route only covers Maths, Science, and Languages (11-16); prompts must confirm current DfE subject list
- "We'll get you a sponsorship licence" upsell: companies charging SA teachers to "find" or "apply for" a UK sponsor school — the Licensed Sponsor Register is publicly searchable for free

## Legitimate programmes / bilateral agreements

- **UK DfE Free QTS Digital Service** (from August 2025): gov.uk official service for overseas-trained teachers; no fees; online application with SACE Letter of Good Standing
- **UK government teacher relocation support**: up to £12,000 for shortage subject teachers recruited from overseas (Maths, Science, Languages, Computing) — verify current amount and eligibility in prompts; programme subject to annual budget
- **AITSL Teacher Skills Assessment**: official Australian government body assessment for teacher migration; fees approximately AUD $595 (primary) / AUD $665 (secondary) — verify on aitsl.edu.au before including in guide
- No formal bilateral SA–UK or SA–AU teaching MRA exists (unlike SAICA engineering Accords); recognition is individual qualification assessment

## Notes for prompt generation

*Domain-specific facts the vault prompts must flag / confirm / avoid:*

- **QTS subject restriction (CRITICAL):** UK DfE overseas QTS route for SA teachers is restricted to subject specialists in Maths, Science, or Languages who are qualified to teach ages 11–16. Primary/Foundation Phase teachers and those in other subjects CAN still teach in UK schools on a Skilled Worker Visa for up to 4 years without QTS, but QTS through the overseas route is not guaranteed. Prompts must research and confirm the current DfE subject list — do not claim all SA teachers get free QTS.
- **SACE Letter of Good Standing pre-condition:** Teacher must have taught in SA for ≥1 year after qualifying. If they left SA immediately after graduating, they cannot obtain the Letter; they may use the SACE Certificate of Registration as an alternative if they have been teaching in a UK school. R400 fee; outstanding SACE annual fees block issuance; issued within 30 days. Prompts must include this nuance.
- **No Youth Mobility Scheme for SA citizens:** Unlike Australian/NZ/Canadian passport holders (under 35), South African citizens have NO working holiday or Youth Mobility Scheme option for the UK. All SA teachers must obtain a Skilled Worker Visa with employer sponsorship, regardless of age. Confirm this in the visa route section.
- **UK salary threshold (time-sensitive):** From September 2025, minimum qualified teacher salary is £32,916 outside London (higher in Fringe/Outer/Inner London). Independent schools may set own pay but must meet Skilled Worker salary threshold. Confirm current thresholds in prompts as these change annually.
- **Australia IELTS note:** Only IELTS Academic accepted for AITSL assessment (not IELTS General, PTE, TOEFL, or OET). SA teachers are native English speakers but must still sit IELTS Academic. Prompts must confirm current AITSL English requirement — study option may waive IELTS.
- **Australia state registration:** After AITSL assessment, teacher must register separately with the state/territory teaching authority (NESA in NSW, VIT in Victoria, QCT in Queensland, etc.). This adds time and cost after arriving. Prompts must flag state-by-state variation as a known research gap.
- **NZ age limit:** Teaching Council of Aotearoa NZ Green List has an age limit of 55 at time of residence visa application. Prompts must include this as a qualification check item.
- **Shared vaults relevant:** wa-shared-documents (SAQA evaluation, SAPS police clearance, apostille via DIRCO) and wa-shared-scams apply to this category. Prompts should reference but not duplicate this content.
- **Salary context for R199 positioning:** UK newly qualified teacher salary starts at £32,916 (outside London). This is approximately R770,000/year vs SA average teacher salary ~R380,000–R450,000. A 2× salary differential is a concrete, compelling fact for the guide introduction.
