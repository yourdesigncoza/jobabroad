## Known scam patterns

Trade-targeted scams in the SA work-abroad space (2024–2026):

- **"Guaranteed UK construction job — pay £R10,000 visa fee"** — fake CoS claims; real UK CoS is assigned by Licensed Sponsor on the public Home Office register.
- **"Australian mining / FIFO crew, no skills assessment needed"** — bypass TRA claim; TRA OSAP is mandatory for skilled migration.
- **Maritime / oil rig employment scams** — Australian High Commission Pretoria has issued public warnings.
- **Fake recruiter on Facebook / WhatsApp** — no verifiable company registration; pressure tactics ("limited spots").
- **"SA trade certificate auto-recognised"** — false; every destination requires assessment (TRA, provincial TEA, EWRB/PGDB).
- **Upfront "training pack" or "registration deposit" before employer named** — legitimate employers pay sponsorship fees; applicants pay only government-published visa fees.
- **Fake apprenticeship-to-PR pipelines** advertised on TikTok/YouTube, especially Canada; real Express Entry / PNP processes are public and codified.
- **"We arrange your CSCS / JIB card before you arrive"** — UK competence cards are issued by the awarding bodies (NOCN, JIB, ECS) directly; agents charging upfront for "guaranteed" cards are red flags.
- **Identity documents requested upfront** for "visa pre-checks" → identity fraud risk.

## Legitimate programmes / bilateral agreements

- **No formal SA–destination bilateral trades labour agreement exists.** Trades are not covered by SAICA/MRA-style mutual recognition or by the Sydney/Dublin Accords (those are engineering-specific).
- **TRA OSAP (Australia)** — designed explicitly for offshore applicants in 5 trade occupations including electrician and plumber (Pathway 1 with practical). Closest thing to a structured SA-friendly assessment programme.
- **Red Seal Program (Canada)** is interprovincial *within Canada*; SA Red Seal is not equivalent — must re-test in destination province.
- **Australian state-nominated 190 / regional 491** — most states publish state-specific Skilled Occupation Lists prioritising trades.
- **Recruitment partnerships:** large UK construction firms (Balfour Beatty, Skanska, Laing O'Rourke), Australian mining/infrastructure (BHP contractors, Multiplex), and NZ residential construction (Fletcher Building) have published international recruitment pages — verifiable on each company's official site, not via agents.

## Notes for prompt generation

- **UK TSL is time-limited.** Every UK section must explicitly state the **31 December 2026 sunset** and warn that no new sponsorships under TSL are guaranteed beyond that date. After the sunset, only RQF 6+ roles remain on the standard Skilled Worker visa — which excludes most trades.
- **UK TSL = no dependants.** Critical user-impact fact; surface prominently in destinations and visa-route sections.
- **UK ILR extended to 10 years from April 2026** (formerly 5) under "earned settlement" model — confirm exact rules at point of writing the visa-routes section.
- **Australian SID Visa replaced TSS 482 on 7 Dec 2024.** Do not reference "TSS" in current-state copy.
- **TRA OSAP practical** is conducted in Brisbane (AU), Leatherhead (UK) or Pampanga (Philippines) — SA applicants typically travel to UK or Philippines; cost and travel must be in the realistic-costs section.
- **TRA fees:** AUD 2,500–3,000 typical full assessment cost; verify current fee on `tradesrecognitionaustralia.gov.au` at write time.
- **SA Red Seal does not transfer to Canada.** Every province re-assesses; surface this clearly — common misconception.
- **Canada Express Entry general draw cutoffs (470–530+) are not realistic for most SA trades.** Steer users to **category-based Trades draws (430–480)** or **PNP**.
