## Known scam patterns

- **Fake J1 agency scams:** Fraudulent "placement agencies" (often Facebook/WhatsApp) claim to issue J1 visas for an upfront fee. Legitimate J1 sponsors are published on the US Dept of State's designated sponsor list (j1visa.state.gov/sponsors); only these organisations can issue the DS-2019. Any agency not on that list is fraudulent.
- **"Guaranteed Disney/resort job" scams:** Promise a specific employer placement (Walt Disney World, Six Flags) in exchange for payment. The J1 programme requires the student to confirm a job offer or use a job-matching service through a legitimate sponsor — no third-party agency can guarantee a specific placement for a fee.
- **Fake UK seasonal recruiter scams:** Target SA students with "UK farm/resort job + visa arranged" offers via Facebook groups; collect upfront fees; no visa is ever processed. UK YMS requires direct application via gov.uk — no agency can "apply on your behalf" for a fee.
- **Upfront fee fraud (generic):** Any "international jobs" service asking for an upfront fee before a contract is signed is a scam. J1 programme fees are paid to licensed sponsors only, are transparently disclosed, and are modest (typically USD $500–1,500 covering programme costs, insurance, SEVIS fee).

## Legitimate programmes / bilateral agreements

- **USA J1 SWT:** US Department of State programme (15 U.S.C. § 2001 et seq.); access via licensed sponsor agencies listed at j1visa.state.gov/sponsors. USIT, CIEE, and STS are the main agencies operating in SA. No SA–USA bilateral agreement required.
- **UK YMS:** Bilateral arrangement between SA and UK included SA in the eligible country list; direct gov.uk application. No ballot required for SA (unlike HK/India/Japan). Annual quota — check gov.uk each year for SA allocation.
- **Canada IEC:** No direct SA–Canada bilateral youth mobility agreement confirmed. SA applicants must use a Recognized Organization (SWAP Working Holidays, GO International) which has been approved by IRCC to nominate non-bilateral-country applicants.

## Notes for prompt generation

- **J1 student-status gate:** J1 SWT is restricted to students currently enrolled full-time at an SA university. This is a hard eligibility gate — vault prompts must make this the first filter question. Non-students are ineligible for J1 SWT and must use UK YMS or Canada IEC instead.
- **Programme timeline is tight:** USA J1 applications for the summer season typically close January–February. The 2025 season closed at the start of February. Vault prompts must flag: "Apply by October–January for the following summer or you will miss the window."
- **Canada IEC limitation:** SA is NOT on the IEC direct eligible list. Prompts must not present IEC as a simple direct application — it requires going through a Recognized Organization. Flag clearly.
- **No PR route:** All three routes are time-limited; none leads to permanent residency. The guide must be honest about this. This is a "first overseas work experience" product, not a migration guide.
- **Age cap UK YMS:** 18–30 strictly. If the reader is 31+ they cannot use YMS. J1 has no specific age cap but requires student status.
- **Savings requirement UK:** £2,500 in an accessible bank account is required at time of application. Prompts in the costs section must include this.
- **J1 costs to disclose:** SEVIS fee USD ~$35; DS-7002 / visa application MRV fee ~USD $160; sponsor programme fee ~USD $500–1,500; travel + accommodation. Total upfront outlay typically ZAR 25,000–40,000.
- **Legitimate sponsor verification:** Vault prompts for scam section must direct users to verify sponsor at j1visa.state.gov/sponsors before paying anyone.
- **Seasonal cycle:** USA J1 runs May–September (Northern Hemisphere summer). UK/Canada permits are year-round. Prompts should note that departure timing matters for USA J1 alignment with SA university semester breaks.
- **OISC/advice boundary:** Seasonal visa info is general information, not regulated immigration advice. All visa eligibility criteria should be sourced from official government pages only.
