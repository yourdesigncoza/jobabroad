**SA body:** **SAICA** — South African Institute of Chartered Accountants (CA(SA) designation; member of IFAC and Global Accounting Alliance). Secondary SA bodies: **SAIPA** (SA Institute of Professional Accountants), **CIMA** (Chartered Institute of Management Accountants — UK-headquartered, recognised globally), **ACCA** (Association of Chartered Certified Accountants — UK-headquartered).

**Note for prompts:** CIMA and ACCA are international-by-design; SA holders of these designations have a different overseas path (direct global recognition of the designation, no SAICA-MRA needed). The MRA-leveraged "CA(SA) overseas" angle is specifically for SAICA members, not CIMA/ACCA. Flag this distinction explicitly in section 1 (Destinations) and section 6 (Contacts).

## Known scam patterns

*Specifically targeting SA accountants and finance professionals:*

- **Fake "SAICA-MRA fast-track" agencies** charging R10k–R30k for paperwork SA CAs can submit themselves to ICAEW/CAANZ/CPA Canada for the official body fee (~£500–£1,000 typical body application fee)
- **Fake London/Dublin recruitment promises** demanding upfront "registration" or "ICAEW conversion" fees before any sponsored job is offered
- **Misrepresentation of AQ (Audit Qualification) requirement** — agencies claiming SAICA = full UK audit signing rights (false; AQ is a separate UK exam route)
- **"CPA Canada in 30 days" promises** ignoring the provincial registration step (CPA Canada is federated; applicants must register provincially)
- **Mauritius offshore-finance "fast-track" recruiters** using SA professional concerns to direct away from regulated EU/UK/AU routes into lower-protection contracts
- **Fake CIMA / ACCA "upgrade" services** targeting CIMA/ACCA-only members (who do not need a SAICA MRA) and selling them MRA services they do not need
- **Generic scams (universal):** WhatsApp-only recruiter profiles; requests for ID/passport copies before contract; "guaranteed placement" claims; Facebook Group-only operators with no UK Companies House / ASIC / NZBN / IRL CRO registration

- **SAICA — NASBA IQEX (Americas)** — renewed April 2026 (recent SAICA news release); enables SA CAs to sit International Qualification Examination for US CPA recognition

- **No formal bilateral SA government work-abroad programme for accountants** — all routes are private/employer-sponsored or skills-based migration

## Notes for prompt generation

*Domain-specific facts the vault prompts must flag, confirm, or avoid:*

- **Time-stamp every salary figure**: UK £41,700 threshold is post-22 July 2025; SOC going rates revised 22 July 2025 from 2024 ASHE — annual review cycle (Confirm each time you cite).
- **UK SOC code precision**: Use SOC 2020 codes (not 2010). 2421 Chartered and Certified Accountants is RQF 6 = qualifies; 3537 Financial and Accounting Technicians is RQF 3 = may NOT qualify after 22 July 2025 RQF 6 rule.
- **AQ (Audit Qualification) is NOT covered by SAICA MRA** — flag this prominently in destination/visa-route sections. SA CAs CAN do all non-audit finance roles; CANNOT sign UK statutory audits without further UK exam.
- **CPA Canada is provincial**: never describe as "register with CPA Canada and you're done" — applicant must register with destination province (CPA Ontario, CPA BC, etc.) after RMA membership is granted.
- **Australia has TWO recognised bodies**: CA ANZ (MRA route) AND CPA Australia (skills-assessment route for migration). Don't conflate.
- **Date-check every MRA**: The 2026 SAICA-NASBA renewal demonstrates these MRAs are time-bound and renewed periodically. Prompts must require evidence_strength = confirmed and cite the MRA destination page directly.
- **CIMA / ACCA distinction**: SA holders of CIMA or ACCA do NOT need SAICA MRAs — their designations are already internationally portable. Flag this in section 1 (Destinations) and section 6 (Contacts) so vault output distinguishes CA(SA) audience from CIMA/ACCA audience.
- **SAIPA limitation**: SAIPA (Professional Accountant SA) does NOT have the MRA network SAICA has. SAIPA members cannot use the MRA shortcut and must follow employer-sponsored / skills-assessment routes from scratch.
- **Mauritius secondary mention**: Mauritius is a known SA-accountant offshore destination; mention as "considered destination outside primary 5" in section 1 only; do not deep-research.
- **Tax-agent registration is separate**: Practising as a tax agent in AU (TPB) and NZ requires separate registration beyond the CA designation — flag in section 4 visa routes for taxation roles.
- **Flag closed/restricted routes**: If UK ACCA route or any other route is closed/restricted, mark `[CLOSED — do not recommend]` per runtime rule.
- **No agent estimates as confirmed figures**: Migration company estimates (Sable, Apostil etc.) are not authoritative for fees — body application fees must come from icaew.com / charteredaccountantsanz.com / cpacanada.ca / cpaireland.ie directly.
- **Ireland CSEP family / Stamp 4 timeline**: 21 months on CSEP → Stamp 4 (which removes employer-tie) → long-term residence after 60 months total; cite enterprise.gov.ie.
- **SA-side document chain**: PCC (SAPS) → DIRCO apostille → SAQA where required → ID/passport — shared across all sections; cross-reference `wa-shared-documents`.
