## 1. Destination Options — Where Can I Actually Go?

South African hospitality workers — chefs, cooks, restaurant managers, hotel supervisors, front-of-house staff — have a narrower set of realistic visa options than nurses or engineers, but several doors are genuinely open. Hospitality is an unregulated occupation in every major destination: there is no equivalent of a nursing board or engineering registration body requiring you to pass an exam before you can work. Recognition runs through the visa pathway, employer sponsorship, and (for Australia) a skills assessment. Your competitive advantage is fluent English, a strong SA service culture, and an established community of SA hospitality professionals already working in UAE, Australian, and UK hospitality.

The single most important fact to know upfront: the UK Skilled Worker visa was significantly narrowed on **22 July 2025**, removing almost all line-level hospitality roles from eligibility. Any recruiter offering UK Skilled Worker sponsorship for a line cook, waiter, bar staff, or kitchen porter position is pitching a route that no longer exists.

### UAE — The Most Accessible Destination for All Levels

The UAE is the most realistic first destination for the widest range of SA hospitality workers. Dubai, Abu Dhabi, and Sharjah have established SA expat hospitality communities and active recruitment by major hotel groups — Marriott, Hilton, Jumeirah Group, and Emaar Hospitality hire SA staff regularly.

**How the visa works:** The UAE Employment Visa (also called UAE Employment Residence Visa) is a 2-year renewable permit tied to a specific employer. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://u.ae/en/information-and-services/jobs/employment-in-the-private-sector/job-offers-and-work-permits-and-contracts/work-permits | path=wiki/UAE Employment Visa.md | claim_id=01-destinations-001 --> The employer must hold a valid UAE trade licence and submit your job offer and contract through MOHRE's Tawtheeq digital system. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://egsh.ae/services/employment-visa | path=wiki/UAE Employment Visa.md | claim_id=01-destinations-002 --> Once approved, a 60-day entry permit is issued. Upon arrival, you complete a medical fitness test, Emirates ID biometric enrolment, and residency visa stamping. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://egsh.ae/services/employment-visa | path=wiki/UAE Employment Visa.md | claim_id=01-destinations-003 --> The General Directorate of Residency and Foreigners Affairs (GDRFA) handles entry permits and residency stamping; the federal ICP (Identity and Citizenship, Customs and Port Security) processes Emirates ID enrolment. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://egsh.ae/services/employment-visa | path=wiki/UAE Employment Visa.md | claim_id=01-destinations-004 --> SA passport holders are fully eligible; no points test or skills assessment is required.

**Zero cost to the worker:** Under MOHRE Resolution No. 47 of 2022, all visa-related costs — work-permit fee, visa fee, Emirates ID, medical fitness test — fall entirely on the employer. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://mohre.gov.ae | path=wiki/UAE Employment Visa.md | claim_id=01-destinations-005 --> Any "registration fee," "visa processing fee," or "placement fee" demanded from you is a federal violation of UAE law, not just a bad deal. This applies whether the demand comes from a recruiter in SA or an agent in Dubai. There is no minimum salary threshold for hospitality roles under the UAE work permit scheme.

**No permanent residence:** The UAE has no permanent residence pathway for standard hospitality workers. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://u.ae/en/information-and-services/jobs/employment-in-the-private-sector/job-offers-and-work-permits-and-contracts/work-permits | path=wiki/UAE Employment Visa.md | claim_id=01-destinations-006 --> The UAE Golden Visa is restricted to investors, exceptional talent, and specific high-tier professionals such as doctors, engineers, and scientists; standard hospitality roles do not qualify. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://u.ae/en/information-and-services/jobs/employment-in-the-private-sector/job-offers-and-work-permits-and-contracts/work-permits | path=wiki/UAE Employment Visa.md | claim_id=01-destinations-007 --> You are on rolling 2-year employment contracts — when employment ends, the visa ends.

**Honest assessment:** The UAE is the right choice for workers who want tax-free earnings, experience on a globally recognised hospitality brand's CV, and a relatively fast pathway to employment. It is not a route for permanent relocation. If you want to settle abroad long-term, treat UAE experience as a stepping stone and build your Australia or Canada plan in parallel.

### Australia — Structured Pathway to Permanent Residence

Australia has a genuine PR pathway for hospitality workers, but it comes with a salary floor that rules out most casual and line-level roles. This is a destination for head chefs and senior cooks whose market salary will realistically clear the threshold.

**The visa:** The Skills in Demand Visa (Subclass 482, Core Skills stream) replaced the former Temporary Skill Shortage visa in December 2024. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://immi.homeaffairs.gov.au/visas/getting-a-visa/visa-listing/skills-in-demand-visa-subclass-482/core-skills-stream | path=wiki/Australia Skills in Demand Visa Subclass 482.md | claim_id=01-destinations-008 --> Chefs (ANZSCO 351311) and Cooks (ANZSCO 351411) are both on the Core Skills Occupation List (CSOL) — confirmed sponsorable occupations. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://immi.homeaffairs.gov.au/visas/getting-a-visa/visa-listing/skills-in-demand-visa-subclass-482/core-skills-stream | path=wiki/Australia Skills in Demand Visa Subclass 482.md | claim_id=01-destinations-009 --> The visa is granted for up to 4 years. After 2 years with an approved sponsor, you can transition to the Subclass 186 Employer Nomination Scheme (Temporary Residence Transition stream) for permanent residence. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://immi.homeaffairs.gov.au/visas/getting-a-visa/visa-listing/skills-in-demand-visa-subclass-482/core-skills-stream | path=wiki/Australia Skills in Demand Visa Subclass 482.md | claim_id=01-destinations-010 -->

**The salary floor:** From 1 July 2025, the Core Skills Income Threshold (CSIT) is AUD $76,515 per year. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://immi.homeaffairs.gov.au/visas/getting-a-visa/visa-listing/skills-in-demand-visa-subclass-482/core-skills-stream | path=wiki/Australia Skills in Demand Visa Subclass 482.md | claim_id=01-destinations-011 --> *This rises to AUD $79,499 from 1 July 2026 — re-verify at immi.homeaffairs.gov.au before lodging any application.* If the Annual Market Salary Rate for your specific role and location is higher than the CSIT, the employer must pay the higher rate. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://immi.homeaffairs.gov.au/visas/getting-a-visa/visa-listing/skills-in-demand-visa-subclass-482/core-skills-stream | path=wiki/Australia Skills in Demand Visa Subclass 482.md | claim_id=01-destinations-012 --> A line cook earning AUD $55,000–$60,000 would not clear the threshold and cannot be sponsored on the Core Skills stream.

**Skills assessment — different authorities for chef vs cook:**
- **Chef (ANZSCO 351311):** assessed by Trades Recognition Australia (TRA). <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://immi.homeaffairs.gov.au/visas/getting-a-visa/visa-listing/skills-in-demand-visa-subclass-482/core-skills-stream | path=wiki/Australia Skills in Demand Visa Subclass 482.md | claim_id=01-destinations-013 -->
- **Cook (ANZSCO 351411):** assessed by VETASSESS (Vocational Education and Training Assessment Services). SA passport holders are in the mandatory assessment group — no 482 can be lodged as a cook without a positive VETASSESS outcome. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.vetassess.com.au/check-my-occupation/trade-occupations/cook-commercial-cookery | path=wiki/VETASSESS.md | claim_id=01-destinations-014 --> The offshore Full Skills Assessment fee is approximately AUD $1,096 *(October 2025 fee schedule — verify current fee at vetassess.com.au before applying, as fees change periodically)*. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.vetassess.com.au/check-my-occupation/trade-occupations/cook-commercial-cookery | path=wiki/VETASSESS.md | claim_id=01-destinations-015 -->

**Honest assessment:** Australia is the best long-term route for a senior hospitality professional who is prepared to invest upfront in the skills assessment and accept the high salary bar. The 482 → 186 pathway is clearly defined. Budget 6–12 months from starting your skills assessment to your first day of work in Australia; it is not a fast move.

### Canada — Employer-Driven with Provincial PR Options

Canada requires a Canadian employer to initiate the process on your behalf. You cannot start the paperwork yourself — the employer must obtain a positive Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA) from Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC) before you can apply for a work permit.

**Eligible occupations:** Chef and cook are separate NOC classifications with different duties and different PR eligibility:

- **Chef — NOC 62200:** Requires planning and directing food preparation across multiple services or outlets; supervisory and kitchen management responsibilities are mandatory. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://noc.esdc.gc.ca | path=wiki/Canada TFWP LMIA.md | claim_id=01-destinations-016 -->
- **Cook — NOC 63200:** Covers line cooks who prepare and cook specific dishes without overall kitchen management responsibility. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://noc.esdc.gc.ca | path=wiki/Canada TFWP LMIA.md | claim_id=01-destinations-017 -->

Do not accept a job offer classified under the wrong NOC — misclassification causes LMIA refusal. If your actual role includes kitchen management and supervision, insist on NOC 62200. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://noc.esdc.gc.ca | path=wiki/Canada TFWP LMIA.md | claim_id=01-destinations-018 -->

**Fees and who pays what:** The employer pays a mandatory LMIA government fee of CAD $1,000 per position; this cost cannot be passed to the worker in any form. <!-- src: tier=secondary | url=https://sobirovs.com/labour-market-impact-assessment-explained-lmia/ | path=wiki/Canada TFWP LMIA.md | claim_id=01-destinations-019 --> The worker pays the work permit application fee and biometrics — approximately CAD $240 combined *(re-verify at canada.ca before applying)*. <!-- src: tier=secondary | url=https://sobirovs.com/labour-market-impact-assessment-explained-lmia/ | path=wiki/Canada TFWP LMIA.md | claim_id=01-destinations-020 --> Any agency requesting LMIA fees or "processing fees" from you as the worker is in violation of federal rules — and of South Africa's Employment Services Act 2014, which prohibits domestic recruiters from charging placement fees.

**Approval reality:** High-wage LMIA applications — salary at or above the provincial median wage — have above 90% approval rates. Low-wage applications face 69–80% refusal rates in 2025–2026. <!-- src: tier=secondary | url=https://sobirovs.com/labour-market-impact-assessment-explained-lmia/ | path=wiki/Canada TFWP LMIA.md | claim_id=01-destinations-021 --> A below-median salary offer is a material risk to the entire process, not just a minor disadvantage.

**PR pathway:** The Alberta Advantage Immigration Program (AAIP) Tourism and Hospitality Stream explicitly includes NOC 62200 and NOC 63200 as eligible occupations. <!-- src: tier=secondary | url=https://seabirdcanadainc.ca/tourism-hospitality-stream | path=wiki/Canada TFWP LMIA.md | claim_id=01-destinations-022 --> Workers must complete 6 consecutive months (780 hours) on an LMIA-based permit in Alberta before applying for provincial nomination. <!-- src: tier=secondary | url=https://seabirdcanadainc.ca/tourism-hospitality-stream | path=wiki/Canada TFWP LMIA.md | claim_id=01-destinations-023 --> British Columbia's PNP also has streams covering hospitality NOCs — verify current stream availability directly at welcomebc.ca before relying on any advice, as streams open and close throughout the year. <!-- src: tier=unverified | url=https://www.welcomebc.ca | path=wiki/Canada TFWP LMIA.md | claim_id=01-destinations-024 -->

**Honest assessment:** Canada rewards patience. The LMIA process is employer-dependent and slow — but the PR pathway via Alberta AAIP is one of the clearest routes to permanent residence available to a SA cook or chef outside of Australia. Tourism-heavy provinces (BC, Alberta, Quebec) have the strongest demand. If you have a credible employer contact in Canada, this route is worth pursuing seriously.

### New Zealand — Open but Requires Further Research

New Zealand has a work visa pathway for chefs — the Accredited Employer Work Visa (AEWV) — and Chef (ANZSCO 351311) appears on the Skilled Migrant Category occupation list, potentially offering a direct permanent residence route. <!-- src: tier=secondary | url=https://visaenvoy.com | path=wiki/New Zealand Accredited Employer Work Visa.md | claim_id=01-destinations-025 --> SA passport holders are eligible for the AEWV. The skill bar reported is NZQF Level 4 equivalent qualification and 5 years' relevant experience. <!-- src: tier=secondary | url=https://visaenvoy.com | path=wiki/New Zealand Accredited Employer Work Visa.md | claim_id=01-destinations-026 -->

The vault research for this destination relied on secondary sources only — no primary Immigration New Zealand data was confirmed during the research period. **Treat New Zealand as a destination that warrants independent verification before committing.** Check current AEWV requirements, salary thresholds, and Skilled Migrant Category points at [immigration.govt.nz](https://www.immigration.govt.nz) before acting on any recruiter or agent advice.

### United Kingdom — Senior Chefs and Catering Managers Only

The UK Skilled Worker route is not closed for hospitality — but it is closed for most hospitality workers. Read this section in full before engaging any UK recruiter.

**What the 22 July 2025 change means:** From 22 July 2025, the Skilled Worker visa was restructured to require degree-level (RQF 6) skills for most occupations, and the general salary threshold was raised from £38,700 to £41,700. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.gov.uk/guidance/immigration-rules/immigration-rules-appendix-skilled-occupations | path=wiki/UK Skilled Worker Visa.md | claim_id=01-destinations-027 --> For hospitality, the following SOC codes are now **ineligible** — no new Skilled Worker sponsorship can be issued for these roles:

- SOC 5435 — Cooks <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/skilled-worker-visa-eligible-occupations/skilled-worker-visa-eligible-occupations-and-codes | path=wiki/UK Skilled Worker Visa.md | claim_id=01-destinations-028 -->
- SOC 9264 — Waiters and Waitresses <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/skilled-worker-visa-eligible-occupations/skilled-worker-visa-eligible-occupations-and-codes | path=wiki/UK Skilled Worker Visa.md | claim_id=01-destinations-029 -->
- SOC 9265 — Bar staff <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/skilled-worker-visa-eligible-occupations/skilled-worker-visa-eligible-occupations-and-codes | path=wiki/UK Skilled Worker Visa.md | claim_id=01-destinations-030 -->
- SOC 9261/9263 — Kitchen and catering assistants <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/skilled-worker-visa-eligible-occupations/skilled-worker-visa-eligible-occupations-and-codes | path=wiki/UK Skilled Worker Visa.md | claim_id=01-destinations-031 -->

**What remains eligible:** SOC 5434 (Chefs — head chef, sous chef, senior chef level) and SOC 5436 (Catering and bar managers) remain on the eligible occupations list as medium-skilled occupations. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/skilled-worker-visa-eligible-occupations/skilled-worker-visa-eligible-occupations-and-codes | path=wiki/UK Skilled Worker Visa.md | claim_id=01-destinations-032 -->

**Salary requirements *(as at May 2026)*:** SOC 5434 applicants must be offered at least £33,400 per year (new-entrant rate) or £41,700 (general threshold), and must also meet the role-specific going rate — whichever is higher applies. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.gov.uk/guidance/immigration-rules/immigration-rules-appendix-skilled-occupations | path=wiki/UK Skilled Worker Visa.md | claim_id=01-destinations-033 --> Re-verify current thresholds at [gov.uk/skilled-worker-visa](https://www.gov.uk/skilled-worker-visa) before applying — salary thresholds have been revised multiple times since 2023.

**What "senior chef" means under SOC 5434:** This is for head chefs, sous chefs, and executive chefs who manage kitchen operations, menus, and food preparation teams. A grill station cook or demi-chef will not meet the SOC 5434 definition and cannot be legally sponsored under it.

**Any recruiter advertising UK Skilled Worker sponsorship for a line cook, kitchen porter, waiter, or barista after July 2025 is selling a non-existent route.** This includes WhatsApp-based "UK chef placement" groups and LinkedIn ads promising "fast UK sponsorship" for general hospitality roles. Certificates of Sponsorship issued for ineligible SOC codes are fraudulent documents; UKVI will cancel the visa on discovery.

**Honest assessment:** The UK is viable only for a narrow tier of SA hospitality workers — experienced senior chefs with provable kitchen management responsibility and a genuine employer sponsor. If you are not in that tier, UAE, Australia, or Canada will give you a faster and more realistic outcome.

### Qatar and Gulf States — Accessible, No Permanence

Qatar operates an employer-sponsored work and residence permit system broadly similar to the UAE. Employer initiates the permit, workers pay no visa fees, and there is no permanent residence pathway. <!-- src: tier=secondary | url=https://remote.com/country-explorer/qatar | path=wiki/Qatar Work Residence Permit.md | claim_id=01-destinations-034 --> The hospitality infrastructure from the 2022 FIFA World Cup continues to employ large numbers of foreign workers. SA passport holders are eligible.

Qatar's kafala system has historically tied workers closely to their employer sponsor, creating power imbalances. Post-2022 labour reforms changed some protections, but their consistent enforcement is not confirmed by primary government sources in this research. Verify current employment protections and worker rights at [adlsa.gov.qa](https://www.adlsa.gov.qa) before accepting any Qatar offer. <!-- src: tier=unverified | url=https://www.adlsa.gov.qa | path=wiki/Qatar Work Residence Permit.md | claim_id=01-destinations-035 -->

### Routes That No Longer Exist

The following are commonly pitched to SA hospitality workers but are not valid immigration pathways:

**UK Skilled Worker for line-level hospitality roles (post-22 July 2025):** SOC 5435 Cooks, SOC 9264 Waiters and Waitresses, SOC 9265 Bar staff, and SOC 9261/9263 Kitchen assistants are all ineligible for Skilled Worker sponsorship. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/skilled-worker-visa-eligible-occupations/skilled-worker-visa-eligible-occupations-and-codes | path=wiki/UK Skilled Worker Visa.md | claim_id=01-destinations-037 --> Any Certificate of Sponsorship offered for these roles after 22 July 2025 is fraudulent.

**USA J-1 Hospitality Intern/Trainee:** The J-1 programme is a cultural exchange route available only to SA students or recent graduates enrolled in culinary or hospitality training programmes. <!-- src: tier=unverified | url=https://j1visa.state.gov | path=wiki/USA J-1 Visa.md | claim_id=01-destinations-038 --> It is not available to established hospitality professionals, and it is not a permanent immigration pathway — J-1 holders must return to SA after the exchange period ends.

**"Come to Dubai on a tourist visa and find a job":** UAE employment requires an employer-initiated work permit before you enter for work purposes. Working on a visit visa is working without authorisation — a deportable offence. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://u.ae/en/information-and-services/jobs/employment-in-the-private-sector/job-offers-and-work-permits-and-contracts/work-permits | path=wiki/UAE Employment Visa.md | claim_id=01-destinations-039 --> Any recruiter advising you to travel to Dubai first and "sort out the paperwork once you are there" is either wrong or running a scam.
