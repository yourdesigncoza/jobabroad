## 2. Document Checklist — What Papers Do I Need?

Farming is a non-regulated occupation in every destination covered in this guide — there is no equivalent of a nursing board, teaching council, or engineering registration body involved. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.dalrrd.gov.za | path=FINAL_REPORT.md | claim_id=02-documents-001 --> What replaces professional registration is employer-issued documentation: reference letters, payslips, UIF records, and IRP5s. These carry full evidential weight in every application. If your work history is informal or poorly documented, the UK and Canada routes are still accessible, but the Australia skilled-stream route is effectively closed to you.

### UK — Seasonal Worker Visa

**The most accessible route for most SA farm workers.** Document burden is low, no professional registration is required, and scheme rules explicitly prohibit charging workers any fees. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.gov.uk/seasonal-worker-visa | path=Documents/UK Certificate of Sponsorship.md | claim_id=02-documents-009 -->

The 2026 allocation is 41,000 horticulture + 1,900 poultry places. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.gov.uk/guidance/seasonal-work-on-farms-guidance-for-workers | path=_BRIEF.md | claim_id=02-documents-010 --> There are five approved scheme operators as of May 2025: AGRI-HR, Concordia (UK) Ltd, Fruitful Jobs, HOPS Labour Solutions Ltd, and Pro-Force Limited — verify the current list at [gov.uk/guidance/seasonal-work-on-farms-guidance-for-workers](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/seasonal-work-on-farms-guidance-for-workers) before applying, as the list changes year-on-year. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.gov.uk/guidance/seasonal-work-on-farms-guidance-for-workers | path=Documents/UK Certificate of Sponsorship.md | claim_id=02-documents-011 --> **Concordia UK is the only operator confirmed to actively recruit from South Africa.** <!-- src: tier=secondary | url=https://www.thegrocer.co.uk/analysis-and-features/why-the-uk-seasonal-labour-worker-scheme-still-isnt-working/698740.article | path=Documents/UK Certificate of Sponsorship.md | claim_id=02-documents-012 --> Fruitful Jobs explicitly excludes all African countries; HOPS recruits from Kenya but not SA; Agri-HR and Pro-Force recruit only from Central Asia and Eastern Europe. <!-- src: tier=secondary | url=https://www.thegrocer.co.uk/analysis-and-features/why-the-uk-seasonal-labour-worker-scheme-still-isnt-working/698740.article | path=Documents/UK Certificate of Sponsorship.md | claim_id=02-documents-013 -->

### USA — H-2A Temporary Agricultural Worker Visa

**Worker document burden is minimal.** The employer drives the entire process and must start it at least 75 days before your intended start date. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.dol.gov/agencies/eta/foreign-labor/programs/h-2a | path=Documents/H-2A Temporary Labor Certification.md | claim_id=02-documents-024 --> If your employer cannot show you a submitted DOL job order (Form ETA-790/790A) at least 75 days before your start date, the H-2A process will not complete in time for your arrival. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.dol.gov/agencies/eta/foreign-labor/programs/h-2a | path=Documents/H-2A Temporary Labor Certification.md | claim_id=02-documents-025 -->

**South Africa was on the H-2A eligible-countries list from November 2024 to November 2025.** <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.uscis.gov | path=FINAL_REPORT.md | claim_id=02-documents-026 --> This designation is renewed annually — verify current SA eligibility at [uscis.gov/newsroom/alerts](https://www.uscis.gov/newsroom/alerts) before pursuing this route. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.uscis.gov | path=FINAL_REPORT.md | claim_id=02-documents-027 --> No police clearance is required for H-2A. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.uscis.gov/i-129 | path=Documents/SAPS Police Clearance Certificate.md | claim_id=02-documents-028 -->

**Employer-side documents — you do not prepare these, but verify the employer has them on schedule:**

**Zero-fee rule:** H-2A federal regulations explicitly prohibit employers and recruiters from charging workers any recruitment, transportation, or visa fees. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.dol.gov/agencies/eta/foreign-labor/programs/h-2a | path=Documents/H-2A Temporary Labor Certification.md | claim_id=02-documents-035 --> The only government cost you bear is the MRV fee (~USD $185), which the employer is required to reimburse once you complete your work term. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.dol.gov/agencies/eta/foreign-labor/programs/h-2a | path=Documents/H-2A Temporary Labor Certification.md | claim_id=02-documents-036 --> Any fee demand from a recruiter is a federal violation and a scam signal.

### Canada — Agricultural Stream LMIA + Work Permit

**Mid-tier document burden.** The employer handles the LMIA application and pays CAD $0 — the LMIA fee is waived entirely for the agricultural stream. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.canada.ca/en/employment-social-development/services/foreign-workers/labour-market-impact-assessment-processing-times.html | path=Documents/Canada LMIA.md | claim_id=02-documents-037 --> Your side of the application requires a solid employment history package — reference letters and payslip records.

**SAWP is closed to South Africans.** Canada's Seasonal Agricultural Worker Program is a bilateral agreement restricted to Mexico and specific Caribbean nations only. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.canada.ca/en/employment-social-development/services/foreign-workers/ | path=Documents/Canada LMIA.md | claim_id=02-documents-038 --> SA applicants must use the Agricultural Stream under the Temporary Foreign Worker Program, not SAWP. If any recruiter references "SAWP placement" for a South African, treat this as a red flag.

LMIA processing averages 16 business days as of May 2025 (figure updated monthly at canada.ca). <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.canada.ca/en/employment-social-development/services/foreign-workers/labour-market-impact-assessment-processing-times.html | path=Documents/Canada LMIA.md | claim_id=02-documents-039 --> The LMIA is valid for 6 months from issue — the worker must submit a work permit application to IRCC before that expiry. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.canada.ca/en/employment-social-development/services/foreign-workers/labour-market-impact-assessment-processing-times.html | path=Documents/Canada LMIA.md | claim_id=02-documents-040 --> Note also that the Canada Agri-Food Pilot closed on 14 May 2025 and is no longer an open route. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/agri-food-pilot.html | path=_BRIEF.md | claim_id=02-documents-041 -->

### Australia — Skilled-Stream Routes (482/491)

**This is a full skilled migration pathway, not a seasonal or entry-level route.** The document burden is materially heavier than UK, H-2A, or Canada. Do not start this process without formal agricultural qualifications or a thoroughly documented employment history, a realistic points score for 491 (minimum 65 points), <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://immi.homeaffairs.gov.au | path=Documents/VETASSESS Skills Assessment.md | claim_id=02-documents-048 --> and an understanding that the process will take months and cost substantially more than any other destination on this list.

**PALM scheme is closed to South Africans.** The Pacific Australia Labour Mobility scheme accepts workers only from 9 Pacific island countries and Timor-Leste. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://palmscheme.gov.au | path=_BRIEF.md | claim_id=02-documents-049 --> Any recruiter pitching "PALM placement" to a South African worker is running a scam — see Section 5.

**Income threshold for 482 Core Skills:** from July 2025, the Temporary Skilled Migration Income Threshold is above AUD $73,150 per year. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://immi.homeaffairs.gov.au | path=Documents/VETASSESS Skills Assessment.md | claim_id=02-documents-050 --> Most entry-level agricultural roles will not reach this threshold. The 482 route is realistically viable for farm managers and experienced livestock supervisors, not general farmhands.

**Before paying for VETASSESS:** confirm your specific occupation by ANZSCO code appears on the current skilled occupation list for your intended visa subclass at [immi.homeaffairs.gov.au/visas/working-in-australia/skills-assessment](https://immi.homeaffairs.gov.au/visas/working-in-australia/skills-assessment). <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://immi.homeaffairs.gov.au | path=Documents/VETASSESS Skills Assessment.md | claim_id=02-documents-051 --> The 491 and 482 use different occupation lists. Paying for a skills assessment before confirming your occupation is listed is a common and expensive mistake.

### Your Next Step

**UK route:** Contact Concordia UK directly at [concordia.org.uk/seasonal-work](https://www.concordia.org.uk/seasonal-work) and register your interest early in the calendar year. Do not pay anyone.

**H-2A (USA):** An accredited US employer must initiate the process — the worker cannot self-apply. Approach through established H-2A placement channels (see Section 6 for contacts). Confirm SA is still on the eligible-countries list at [uscis.gov](https://www.uscis.gov/newsroom/alerts) before investing time.

**Canada:** Confirm your employer is applying under the Agricultural Stream LMIA (not SAWP) and request the LMIA file number once approved. Prepare your employment reference letters and payslip records now.

**Australia skilled stream:** Check your occupation ANZSCO code against the current occupation list at [immi.homeaffairs.gov.au](https://immi.homeaffairs.gov.au/visas/working-in-australia/skills-assessment) before spending anything on VETASSESS or an English test.
