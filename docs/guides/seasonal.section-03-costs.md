## 3. Realistic Costs — How Much Will This Actually Cost Me?

All ZAR figures below use May 2026 exchange rates (USD$1 = R16.63). <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.resbank.co.za | path=FINAL_REPORT.md | claim_id=03-costs-001 --> Exchange rates move — treat ZAR equivalents as planning estimates, not guarantees. All USD figures are confirmed from official US government or primary programme sources.

### Read This First: Only One Route Is Open to South Africans

Two programmes are regularly advertised to South African students — the UK Youth Mobility Scheme (YMS) and Canada's International Experience Canada (IEC) Working Holiday. Neither is available to South African passport holders.

- **UK YMS:** South Africa is not listed among the eligible nationalities on gov.uk for 2025/2026. No bilateral Youth Mobility Agreement between SA and the UK exists. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.gov.uk/youth-mobility | path=FINAL_REPORT.md | claim_id=03-costs-002 -->
- **Canada IEC:** South Africa is not in Canada's bilateral IEC country list. Recognised Organisations such as SWAP Working Holidays and GO International restrict their nomination programmes to citizens of IEC-eligible countries only. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/work-canada/iec/eligibility/eligibility-by-country.html | path=wiki/Cost Items/Canada IEC Government Application Fee.md | claim_id=03-costs-003 -->

**The one programme South Africans can actually access: the J-1 Summer Work Travel (SWT) programme, operated by the US Department of State.** No bilateral agreement is required. Access is through a licensed sponsor agency. All cost figures in this section are J-1 SWT only.

---

### Eligibility Gate — Confirm Before Spending Anything

The J-1 SWT programme has one hard eligibility condition that no amount of money can bypass: **you must be currently enrolled full-time at a South African university.** <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://j1visa.state.gov/programs/summer-work-travel/ | path=FINAL_REPORT.md | claim_id=03-costs-004 --> This is a student exchange programme. Non-students are ineligible and have no equivalent seasonal work route to the USA.

If you are not currently enrolled full-time, stop here. The UK and Canada routes are not open to you either. This guide will not be useful until your enrolment status changes.

### The Three Cost Layers

Every J-1 applicant faces three layers of costs before earning a single dollar.

#### Layer 1 — US Government Fees (Fixed for All Applicants)

These fees do not vary by sponsor, employer, or location. They are paid directly to US government agencies.

| Fee | USD amount | ZAR equivalent | Paid to |
|---|---|---|---|
| SEVIS I-901 fee | $220 | R3,659 | SEVP via fmjfee.com |
| MRV visa interview fee (DS-160) | $185 | R3,077 | US Embassy Pretoria or Consulate Cape Town |
| Visa reciprocity fee | $0 | R0 | — |
| **Total government fees** | **$405** | **R6,736** | |

<!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.fmjfee.com | path=FINAL_REPORT.md | claim_id=03-costs-005 -->

Two common figures found online are outdated. The SEVIS fee is currently **$220**, not the $35 sometimes cited — the $35 rate was discontinued years ago. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.fmjfee.com | path=FINAL_REPORT.md | claim_id=03-costs-006 --> The MRV interview fee is currently **$185**, raised from $160 in 2024. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://za.usembassy.gov/visas/nonimmigrant-visas/ | path=FINAL_REPORT.md | claim_id=03-costs-007 --> The reciprocity fee is confirmed at $0: South Africa is on the US government's reciprocity schedule as having no issuance fee for J visa holders on US government-sponsored exchange programmes. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/us-visas/Visa-Reciprocity-and-Civil-Documents-by-Country/SouthAfrica.html | path=FINAL_REPORT.md | claim_id=03-costs-008 -->

#### Layer 2 — Sponsor Programme Fee

Your licensed sponsor issues the DS-2019 Certificate of Eligibility — without this document you cannot book a visa interview. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://j1visa.state.gov/programs/summer-work-travel/ | path=FINAL_REPORT.md | claim_id=03-costs-009 --> The fee is paid to your sponsor before departure and varies based on whether job placement is included.

| Tier | USD amount | ZAR equivalent | Typical inclusion |
|---|---|---|---|
| Self-placed (you find your own employer) | $500 | R8,315 | DS-2019, orientation, insurance bundled, admin |
| Partial placement support | $900 | R14,967 | Above + sponsor assists with employer matching |
| Full placement (employer pre-confirmed) | $1,500 | R24,945 | All above + employer matched and confirmed before departure |

<!-- src: tier=secondary | url=https://www.ciee.org/in-the-usa/work/work-travel-usa/fee-disclosure | path=wiki/Cost Items/CIEE Work Travel USA Programme Fee.md | claim_id=03-costs-010 -->

CIEE is one of the largest US DoS-designated sponsors. Other sponsors — STS, InterExchange, BUNAC/IENA — charge fees in the same range. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://j1visa.state.gov/sponsors/ | path=FINAL_REPORT.md | claim_id=03-costs-011 --> Always request an itemised fee breakdown before paying: confirm whether SEVIS ($220) and health insurance are bundled into the sponsor fee or listed separately. Some sponsors quote a lower headline fee but add these items on top.

Do not pay any sponsor fee to an agency that is not listed on the US Department of State's official sponsor directory at [j1visa.state.gov/sponsors](https://j1visa.state.gov/sponsors). <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://j1visa.state.gov/sponsors/ | path=FINAL_REPORT.md | claim_id=03-costs-012 --> Unlisted agencies cannot issue a DS-2019. Payment to them is a scam. See Section 5 for red flags.

#### Layer 3 — Pre-Departure Out-of-Pocket

**Flights:**

Return economy airfare, Johannesburg to Orlando (MCO), ranges from approximately R20,000 (advance booking) to R30,000 (last-minute or high-demand period). <!-- src: tier=unverified | url=https://www.google.com/flights | path=FINAL_REPORT.md | claim_id=03-costs-013 --> J-1 applicants who confirm placements in January typically depart in May — book as soon as you receive your DS-2019. Prices rise sharply from March onward.

**Housing deposit (Disney placements):**

If placed at Walt Disney World, on-site housing at Flamingo Crossings Village costs USD $227–$275 per week, automatically deducted from your paycheck. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://support.disneyprograms.com/hc/en-us/articles/360004149572-What-are-the-Fees-for-a-Disney-Program | path=wiki/Cost Items/Flamingo Crossings Village Accommodation.md | claim_id=03-costs-014 --> A pre-arrival deposit of USD $227 (R3,775) is required approximately three weeks before your start date — this is a real cash cost paid before any wages begin. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://support.disneyprograms.com/hc/en-us/articles/360004149572-What-are-the-Fees-for-a-Disney-Program | path=wiki/Cost Items/Flamingo Crossings Village Accommodation.md | claim_id=03-costs-015 --> Disney does not provide free housing — it provides employer-facilitated housing at a programme rate, deducted from your wages.

Non-Disney placements (resort hotels, national parks, amusement parks) vary. Budget a similar deposit range unless your employer confirms otherwise in writing.

**First-month living (excl. housing):**

Groceries, local transport, phone SIM, and incidentals during your first month: estimate USD $300–$600 (R5,000–R9,978) depending on location and personal habits. <!-- src: tier=unverified | url=https://www.numbeo.com/cost-of-living/in/Orlando | path=FINAL_REPORT.md | claim_id=03-costs-016 -->

### What You Will Earn — and Whether You Break Even

**Florida minimum wage** as of September 2025: USD $14.00/hour, rising to USD $15.00/hour in September 2026. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://floridajobs.org/workforce-statistics/publications-and-reports/labor-market-information-reports/wages/minimum-wage | path=FINAL_REPORT.md | claim_id=03-costs-022 --> Florida has no state income tax. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://floridarevenue.com/taxes/Pages/default.aspx | path=FINAL_REPORT.md | claim_id=03-costs-023 -->

**Weekly net earnings at Disney World (illustrative):**

| Item | USD | ZAR |
|---|---|---|
| Gross wages ($14.00 × 32h) | $448 | R7,450 |
| Less: Flamingo Crossings housing (mid-rate) | −$250 | −R4,158 |
| Less: estimated federal income tax | −$45 | −R748 |
| **Weekly net take-home** | **~$153** | **~R2,544** |
| Monthly net (4 weeks) | **~$612** | **~R10,178** |

<!-- src: tier=secondary | url=https://support.disneyprograms.com/hc/en-us/articles/360004149572-What-are-the-Fees-for-a-Disney-Program | path=wiki/Cost Items/Flamingo Crossings Village Accommodation.md | claim_id=03-costs-024 -->

**Break-even reality:** At ~R10,178/month net and mid-scenario pre-departure costs of R56,513, you would need approximately 5.5 months of net income to fully recover your investment. <!-- src: tier=secondary | url=https://FINAL_REPORT.md | path=FINAL_REPORT.md | claim_id=03-costs-025 --> The J-1 SWT programme runs May–September — roughly 4 months. This means **most participants do not fully recover their pre-departure costs from J-1 wages alone** at standard hours.

What changes the maths:
- **Overtime** — Disney and resort employers regularly offer hours above 32/week; at $14–$15/hour each extra hour improves the return after the housing deduction is already covered
- **Tips** — food and beverage service roles commonly attract gratuity; not guaranteed but common
- **Low spending** — Disney provides free transport between Flamingo Crossings and park, reducing daily commute costs

<!-- src: tier=secondary | url=https://www.ciee.org/in-the-usa/work/work-travel-usa | path=wiki/Cost Items/Flamingo Crossings Village Accommodation.md | claim_id=03-costs-101 -->

The honest framing: J-1 SWT is primarily a career development and cultural exchange programme. Participants who go in expecting to bank R100,000+ are disappointed. Those who go in expecting to roughly break even on cash while gaining US work experience, international references, and a credential on their CV get full value. <!-- src: tier=unverified | url=https://FINAL_REPORT.md | path=FINAL_REPORT.md | claim_id=03-costs-026 -->

### What the Programme Does Not Cover

| Cost item | Covered? |
|---|---|
| Sponsor programme fee | No — you pay before departure |
| SEVIS + MRV visa fees | No — paid directly to US government |
| Return flights | No — your cost (confirm with employer if any contribution is offered) |
| Pre-arrival housing deposit | No — cash out-of-pocket before arrival |
| Health insurance | Bundled in most sponsor packages — confirm it meets J-1 visa insurance requirements |
| Ongoing housing in USA | Deducted from paycheck — not free |
| Meals and groceries | Not covered — budget USD $200–$300/month |

### Your Number: What to Have in Your Account Before You Fly

**Minimum liquid savings required before departure: R45,000–R60,000.** <!-- src: tier=secondary | url=https://FINAL_REPORT.md | path=FINAL_REPORT.md | claim_id=03-costs-028 -->

This covers sponsor fee (low to mid), all US government visa fees, return flight (booked in advance), pre-arrival housing deposit, and a first-month living buffer. <!-- src: tier=secondary | url=https://www.ciee.org/in-the-usa/work/work-travel-usa/fee-disclosure | path=wiki/Cost Items/CIEE Work Travel USA Programme Fee.md | claim_id=03-costs-102 -->

If you have less than R45,000 saved, apply for the following summer's programme and use the intervening months to save. Applications for the Northern Hemisphere summer (May–September) typically open September–October and close January–February of the following year. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://j1visa.state.gov/programs/summer-work-travel/ | path=FINAL_REPORT.md | claim_id=03-costs-029 --> Missing the February window means waiting a full year.
