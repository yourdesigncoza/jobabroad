### USA — H-2A Temporary Agricultural Worker Visa

H-2A carries the highest farm wages of any destination. The key legal point: the employer — not the worker — is responsible for recruitment, transport, and housing costs.

#### Government fees and the reimbursement rule

| Item | Amount | Who pays | Reimbursable? |
|---|---|---|---|
| DS-160 MRV visa fee | $205 (R3,415) | Worker advances | Yes — 20 CFR § 655.122(p) <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/us-visas/visa-information-resources/fees/fees-visa-services.html | path=Cost Items/USA H-2A DS-160 MRV Fee.md | claim_id=03-costs-016 --> |
| CBP I-94 entry fee (air travel) | $24 (R400) | Worker advances | Yes — 20 CFR § 655.122(p); fee introduced October 2025 <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.federalregister.gov | path=Cost Items/USA H-2A CBP I-94 Border Fee.md | claim_id=03-costs-017 --> |
| DHS visa integrity fee | $250 minimum (R4,165) | Unclear | Unresolved — employer reimbursement obligation under review; confirm in your contract <!-- src: tier=secondary | url=https://www.uscis.gov | path=FINAL_REPORT.md | claim_id=03-costs-018 --> |
| Recruitment / placement / referral fee | **$0 — prohibited** | — | Any fee request is a legal violation |
| I-129 petition fee (USCIS) | Employer-borne | Employer only | Cannot be passed to worker |
| Transport from South Africa to worksite | Employer-borne | 20 CFR § 655.122(h) <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-20/chapter-V/part-655/subpart-B/section-655.122 | path=Cost Items/Worker Fee Prohibition H-2A.md | claim_id=03-costs-019 --> | Mandatory |
| Return transport after contract | Employer-borne | 20 CFR § 655.122(h) | Mandatory |
| Worksite housing | Employer-borne | 20 CFR § 655.122(d) <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-20/chapter-V/part-655/subpart-B/section-655.122 | path=Cost Items/Worker Fee Prohibition H-2A.md | claim_id=03-costs-020 --> | Mandatory; must meet federal safety standards |

Under 20 CFR § 655.135(j)–(k), H-2A employers and their agents are **prohibited from charging workers** any recruitment, referral, placement, or labour-certification fees. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-20/chapter-V/part-655/subpart-B/section-655.135 | path=Cost Items/Worker Fee Prohibition H-2A.md | claim_id=03-costs-021 --> Under 20 CFR § 655.122(p), the employer must pay or reimburse the worker in their first paycheck for all visa fees and government-mandated border charges. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-20/chapter-V/part-655/subpart-B/section-655.122 | path=Cost Items/Worker Fee Prohibition H-2A.md | claim_id=03-costs-022 -->

**Interview waivers discontinued.** From September 2, 2025, all H-2A applicants must attend an in-person consular interview — typically at the US Consulate in Johannesburg. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://travel.state.gov | path=FINAL_REPORT.md | claim_id=03-costs-023 --> Budget travel costs to the consulate.

#### Upfront advance required (before employer reimbursement)

In practice, workers must advance visa fees, the consulate trip, and personal costs before the first reimbursement paycheck arrives.

| Scenario | Upfront advance (before reimbursement) |
|---|---|
| **Low** | ~R38,000 |
| **High** | ~R55,000 |

<!-- src: tier=secondary | url= | path=FINAL_REPORT.md | claim_id=03-costs-024 -->

After reimbursement, your net cost for visa fees and transport should be near zero. You must have the advance available before departure. If any recruiter demands payment for recruitment, training, registration, or placement, that is a violation of US federal law and a trafficking red flag — not a normal cost. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-20/chapter-V/part-655/subpart-B/section-655.135 | path=Cost Items/Worker Fee Prohibition H-2A.md | claim_id=03-costs-025 -->

**Wages:** H-2A employers must pay at least the Adverse Effect Wage Rate (AEWR) — a DOL-set floor that protects both H-2A and domestic workers. Most US states' AEWRs fall between $17 and $20/hour as of 2025. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://flag.dol.gov/wage-data/adverse-effect-wage-rates | path=H-2A Adverse Effect Wage Rate.md | claim_id=03-costs-026 --> At $17/hour × 40 hours/week, gross monthly earnings are approximately $2,720 (R45,288). Check the current state-by-state AEWR at [flag.dol.gov](https://flag.dol.gov/wage-data/adverse-effect-wage-rates) before accepting any offer.

### Tax Note

Working abroad does not automatically end your South African tax residency. The 183-day rule does not make you a non-resident — it exempts foreign employment income only up to R1.25m/year under section 10(1)(o)(ii) of the Income Tax Act. <!-- src: tier=primary | url=https://www.sars.gov.za | path=wa-shared-tax-exchange/wiki/Foreign Employment Income Exemption.md | claim_id=03-costs-050 --> If you complete a 6-month UK season and return to South Africa, you remain SA tax-resident and must declare your UK earnings on your annual tax return. Confirm your position with a SARS-registered tax adviser before departure.
