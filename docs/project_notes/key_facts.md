## Legal Position

Jobabroad is legally safe as an information-only product in all 6 jurisdictions. The universal safe harbour: content not tailored to a specific named individual = not regulated advice anywhere.

| Jurisdiction | Regulatory test | SA-based provider subject? | Max penalty |
|---|---|---|---|
| UK (IAA) | "relates to a particular individual" (IAA 1999 s.82) | No — UK-only scope | 2yr + unlimited fine; £15k civil (BSA 2025) |
| Australia (MARA) | "assist a visa applicant" with their application (s.276) | Likely no — no explicit extraterritorial | ~AUD $16,500 |
| NZ (IAA NZ) | "advise, direct, assist, or represent a person" | **Yes — explicitly extraterritorial** | 7yr + NZD $100,000 |
| Canada (CICC) | "represent or advise a person for consideration" (IRPA s.91) | **Yes — explicitly extraterritorial** | CAD $200,000 + 2yr |
| SA | None (s.46 repealed 2014) | N/A — unrestricted | None |
| Ireland | None (no immigration adviser regulation) | N/A — unrestricted | None |

## Nursing Destination Routes — Critical Facts (2025/2026)

> These correct widespread misinformation in online guides. Verify before using in any content.

**AHPRA pathway for SA nurses is NOT 4–6 weeks.**
SA is not among AHPRA's "approved countries" for Pathway 1 (streamlined) — those countries are UK, Ireland, US, British Columbia, Ontario, Singapore, and Spain only. SA nurses must use the Standard OBA (Objective-Based Assessment) Pathway, which involves: Self-check → Stream assignment (A/B/C) → qualification assessment → portfolio + exams if Stream B → two-part Orientation → registration. Timeline: **3–6 months**. April 2025 AHPRA update on "comparable jurisdictions" faster processing also excludes SA.

**SA R425 training is comprehensively broader than most source countries.**
Regulation R425 covers all four nursing domains in one qualification: general nursing, psychiatric nursing, community nursing, and midwifery. Most competing source countries train nurses in a single specialty. Additionally, all SA nurses complete a mandatory 1-year community service year in a public health facility before SANC registration — giving them verifiable primary care experience. This makes SA nurses eligible for Mental Health Services and Community Services placements in Ireland (KCR framework) without additional psychiatric credentials.

**Ireland HSE relocation package (2025 confirmed figures):**
- Flight to Ireland — reimbursed
- Temporary accommodation — up to 4 weeks on arrival
- Relocation allowance — €1,500 to €3,000 (varies by circumstances)
- NMBI registration fees, work permit, RCSI adaptation programme — covered by HSE
- Starting salary — €32,000–€38,000/year + shift/overtime allowances
- Total recruitment-to-start timeline: 3–6 months
- Ireland shortage: ~4,000 nurses as of 2025 (INMO member survey)
- SA confirmed as primary non-Indian source country (Servisource: "India and South Africa" = 80% of overseas placements, 2019)
- IELTS requirement is waived for SA nurses (native English speakers) — not stated on every agency site but confirmed by NMBI rules

**Ireland recruits for community/geriatric nursing, not just acute hospitals.**
KCR (Ireland's #1 HSE framework agency) places nurses in: General Medical/Surgical, ICU, Theatre, A&E, CCU, Cardiology, Oncology, Orthopaedics, Renal, Radiology, Elderly Medicine, Paediatrics, Midwifery, Neonatal, Gynaecology, Mental Health Services, Community Services, Intellectual Disability Services.

**NZ Green List Tier 1 = Straight to Residence (fastest PR of all destinations).**
Registered nursing is on Tier 1 — an accredited NZ employer can offer permanent residency from day one. No points test. No temporary visa intermediate step. This is structurally faster than Australia (points-tested), UK (10-year ILR), or Ireland (Stamp 4 after 2 years). NCNZ assessment takes 3–4 months for SA nurses.

**Canada NCLEX-RN: IEN first-time pass rate is 54.5% (Q3 2024).**
This is the decisive Canada barrier. Internationally educated nurses pass at significantly lower rates than Canadian-educated nurses. SA nurses planning the Canada route must budget for 3–6 months of dedicated NCLEX-RN preparation. NNAS Expedited (5-day) reports now accepted by Ontario (CNO) and Alberta (CRNA) as of 2025. Express Entry CRS scores for healthcare draws were volatile in 2024–25 (422–510 range).

**NMC→AHPRA Strategy (UK-first shortcut):**
Register NMC → work 1,800 hours NHS → qualify for AHPRA Pathway 1 (1–3 months, no exam in Australia). Total ~15–21 months but eliminates AU exam costs. SA nurses cannot access AHPRA Pathway 1 by registering directly from SA (SA not on approved comparable jurisdictions list post April 2025 update).

**SA nursing workforce context (source country):**
SA had 1.3 nurses per 1,000 population in 2017 (WHO recommendation: 4.45). Projected deficit of 34,000 nurses by 2025. SA is NOT on the WHO Health Workforce Safeguards List — active recruitment of SA nurses is legally and ethically permitted by all destination countries. This distinguishes SA nurses from Zimbabwe, Zambia, and 37 other listed countries whose nurses cannot be actively recruited.

**Midwifery note:**
SA R425 includes a midwifery component but does NOT automatically qualify a nurse to register as a standalone midwife overseas. Overseas midwifery registers (NMBI Midwives Register, NMBA via AHPRA) require the midwifery component to meet their standalone midwifery education standards (supervised births, antenatal care hours). Nurses with a dedicated SANC midwifery registration or qualification are better positioned for midwifery-specific placements.
