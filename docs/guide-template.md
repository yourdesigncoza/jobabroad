# Category Guide Template

Each profession guide answers the five questions a buyer is actually asking:
- How do I actually get there?
- What papers do I need?
- How much will it cost me?
- Who do I contact?
- Will I get scammed?

---

## Guide Structure

### 1. Destination Options
*Which countries are realistic for this field?*

- List 2–4 destinations with active demand for SA professionals in this field
- For each destination: current demand signal, SA diaspora size, why it's realistic
- Honest about which routes are closed or unlikely (e.g. care workers UK July 2025)
- Source every claim — GOV.UK, DETE, Home Affairs, AHPRA, etc.

### 2. Step-by-Step Document Checklist
*What papers do I need?*

- Ordered list from SA-side prep through to destination-side submission
- Flag which documents require lead time (SAQA evaluation, SAPS clearance, apostille)
- Note SA regulatory body involvement (SANC, SACE, SAICA, ECSA) where applicable
- Cross-reference shared node: [SA Document Universals]

### 3. Realistic Costs
*How much will it cost me?*

- Visa application fees (per destination)
- Professional registration fees (NMC, AHPRA, AITSL, etc.)
- Document costs (SAQA, apostille, police clearance, language test)
- Flight estimate (ZAR, return)
- Relocation estimate (first month: accommodation + settling-in costs)
- Total range: low / mid / high scenario
- Note: what the employer typically covers vs what comes out of pocket

### 4. Visa Route Overview
*What's the actual process?*

- Named visa route with current official status
- Eligibility requirements (qualifications, salary threshold, language, age)
- Processing time (realistic, not best-case)
- PR pathway and timeline
- Any recent changes or upcoming reforms to flag
- One official link per route (Home Office, DETE, Home Affairs, etc.)

### 5. Scam Red Flags
*Will I get scammed?*

- Universal red flags (cross-reference shared node: [Universal Scam Red Flags])
- Field-specific scam patterns for this profession
- How to verify a legitimate employer: point to the public register (UK Licensed Sponsor Register, DETE permit check, etc.)
- What legitimate programmes never ask for upfront

### 6. Legitimate Contacts & Official Links
*Who do I contact?*

- Destination government / visa portals (official links only)
- Destination professional regulator (NMC, AHPRA, NMBI, etc.)
- SA professional body (SANC, SACE, SAICA, ECSA)
- Vetted SA migration support companies (Sable, Apostil — with scope clarity)
- Any official SA government programme or bilateral agreement relevant to this field

## Content Standards

- Every factual claim links to an official government, regulator, or programme source
- No blog posts, no hearsay, no agent estimates
- Costs are ranges, not guarantees
- Date-stamp any volatile data (visa fees, salary thresholds, occupation lists) — these change
- Tone: direct, no fluff, written for someone who has been burned before or fears being burned
